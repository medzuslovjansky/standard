Sample Texts and Communication Scenarios

This appendix presents annotated sample texts drawn from authentic Interslavic sources. Each entry provides the text in standard Latin orthography and an English translation. Brief grammar notes highlight features that recur across the Interslavic system.

Sources: dialogues adapted from the Ranmaru educational series (files 56–74); prose relay texts compiled by Jan van Steenbergen.

Moj najlěpši prijatelj​

Topic: Describing a friend; personal appearance and character

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Moj najlěpši prijatelj
AImaš li najlěpšego prijatelja?
BRåzuměje sę!
AKako je jego imę?
BImę mu je Miša.
AKako on izględa?
BOn je vysoky. Ima světle vlåsy i modre oči. Mysljų, že on je veljmi lěpy.
AZačto on ti sę podoba?
BOn je istinny optimist. Ako li imam problem, on vsegda vě, čto kazati. On je veljmi spokojny. Takože ima odličny smysl za humor.
AKoliko dòlgo vy sę poznate?
BPoznamo sę uže mnogo lět. Studovali jesmo zajedno.

English translation​

LineEnglish
My best friend
ADo you have a best friend?
BOf course!
AWhat is his name?
BHis name is Misha.
AWhat does he look like?
BHe is tall. He has fair hair and blue eyes. I think he is very good-looking.
AWhy do you like him?
BHe is a true optimist. If I have a problem, he always knows what to say. He is very calm. He also has an excellent sense of humor.
AHow long have you known each other?
BWe have known each other for many years already. We studied together.

Grammar notes. The superlative najlěpši (best) uses the prefix naj- added to the comparative lěpši. The reflexive particle sę in poznate sę and Råzuměje sę signals a mutual or impersonal action. Imę mu je Miša uses the dative of interest (mu) to express possession: "his name is Misha" (literally, "the name to-him is Misha"). Mysljų is first-person singular present of mysljati (to think).

Obyčny denj​

Topic: Daily routine; present tense narration

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Obyčny denj
AČto ty dělaš každy denj utrom?
BJa ljubjų vstavati rano. Ja vsegda vstajų v 7 časov i běgam v parku. Podčas běga, obyčno ja slušam muzykų na mp3. Potom sę myjų pod dušem i zautrakajų.
AČto ty obyčno imaš za zautrak?
BObyčno ja pijų čaj i jěm sendvič. Poněkda, kògda imam malo vrěmena, ja samo pijų kavų s mlěkom.
AJězdiš li na råbotų avtom?
BNe, prědvyšam pųtovati metrom.
AKdě råbotaš i čto dělaš?
BJa råbotam v kompaniji. Každy denj råzgovarjam s klientami i odpovědam na e-maily. Moja råbota ne je veljmi zanimliva.
AČto dělaš poslě råboty?
BPoslě råboty idų do doma. Doma kuham večerjų, a potom ględam noviny ili čitam knigų.

English translation​

LineEnglish
An ordinary day
AWhat do you do every morning?
BI love to get up early. I always get up at 7 o'clock and jog in the park. While jogging I usually listen to music on my mp3 player. Then I shower and have breakfast.
AWhat do you usually have for breakfast?
BI usually drink tea and eat a sandwich. Sometimes, when I have little time, I only drink coffee with milk.
ADo you go to work by car?
BNo, I prefer to travel by metro.
AWhere do you work and what do you do?
BI work in a company. Every day I talk with clients and answer e-mails. My work is not very interesting.
AWhat do you do after work?
BAfter work I go home. At home I cook dinner, and then I watch the news or read a book.

Grammar notes. Poněkda (sometimes) and podčas (during, while) are common adverbial time expressions. The verb prědvyšati (to prefer) takes an infinitive complement. Doma (at home) functions as a locative adverb without a preposition. Accusatives with motion prepositions: na råbotų, do doma.

Svobodno vrěmę​

Topic: Leisure activities; weekends

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Svobodno vrěmę
AČto ty dělaš v svobodno vrěmę?
BV svobodno vrěmę ja ljubjų hoditi v basen. Mi sę podoba plavati. Ovo je dobro za zdråvje.
AHodiš li sama ili s prijateljami?
BMoji prijatelji ne ljubęt plavati. Zato obyčno hođų sama.
AA čto ty ljubiš dělati vikendom?
BV vikend ljubjų dòlgo spati, ibo v tečeńju tydnja vsegda vstajų veljmi rano. Takože mi sę podoba hoditi v fitness klub. V sobotų strěćam sę s prijateljami i idemo na diskotekų.
AA čto ty ljubiš dělati v nedělji?
BV nedělji ja ljubjų odpočivati. Mi sę podoba ględati televizijų ili čitati knigų. Veljmi ljubjų takože teatr i kino. Nedělja je idealny denj, da sę odpravim v kino.

English translation​

LineEnglish
Free time
AWhat do you do in your free time?
BIn my free time I love going to the swimming pool. I enjoy swimming. It is good for one's health.
ADo you go alone or with friends?
BMy friends don't like to swim. So I usually go alone.
AAnd what do you like to do at weekends?
BAt the weekend I like to sleep in late, because during the week I always get up very early. I also enjoy going to the fitness club. On Saturday I meet up with friends and we go to a disco.
AAnd what do you like to do on Sunday?
BOn Sunday I like to relax. I enjoy watching TV or reading a book. I also really love the theatre and cinema. Sunday is the ideal day to go to the cinema.

Grammar notes. Mi sę podoba (I enjoy / it pleases me) uses the dative subject mi — the verb agrees with the infinitive or noun, not with the experiencer. Zato (therefore, that is why) is a causal connective. Da sę odpravim introduces a purpose clause using da + present subjunctive.

Odkųdy jesi?​

Topic: Meeting someone new; origins and language learning

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Odkųdy jesi?
AAhoj! Jesi li nova?
BAhoj! Da, dneś je moj pŕvy denj v školě. Kako sę zòveš?
AZòvų sę Stefani. A kako ty sę zòveš?
BZòvų sę Marta. Prijatno mi je tę poznati. Odkųdy jesi?
AJesm iz SŠA. A ty?
BJesm iz Išpanije. Žijų ovdě uže 2 měsęca.
AVeljmi dobro govoriš russky.
BDękujų. Moja najlěpša prijateljka je rusinka i ona mę uči.
AUdačnica. Može byti, tòž trěbam russkogo prijatelja, da byh lučše govorila.

English translation​

LineEnglish
Where are you from?
AHi! Are you new here?
BHi! Yes, today is my first day at school. What is your name?
AMy name is Stefani. And what is your name?
BMy name is Marta. Nice to meet you. Where are you from?
AI am from the USA. And you?
BI am from Spain. I have been living here for 2 months already.
AYou speak Russian very well.
BThank you. My best friend is Russian and she teaches me.
AYou are lucky. Maybe I also need a Russian friend to speak better.

Grammar notes. Zòvų sę (my name is; I am called) is the reflexive construction using first-person singular of zòvati (to call). Trěbati (to need) here governs a genitive object (russkogo prijatelja). The conditional da byh govorila uses the auxiliary byh (conditional first-person singular) plus the l-participle.

Pivo, prošų​

Topic: Ordering in a bar; asking for help

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Pivo, prošų (V baru)
ADajte mi dva piva, prošų.
BKakòvo pivo? Temno ili světlo?
AČto? Žalj mi je, ja ne govorjų russky. Samo pivo.
BPa, sdaje sę mi, že vy govorite russky.
AGovorjų trohų i ne råzumějų, čto vy pytate.
BKakòvo pivo? Ukažite pŕstom.
AOvo za 50 rubljev.
BDobro. Dva piva. Bųde 100 rubljev.

English translation​

LineEnglish
A beer, please (In a bar)
AGive me two beers, please.
BWhat kind of beer? Dark or light?
AWhat? I'm sorry, I don't speak Russian. Just beer.
BWell, it seems to me that you do speak Russian.
AI speak a little and I don't understand what you are asking.
BWhat kind of beer? Point with your finger.
AThis one for 50 roubles.
BFine. Two beers. That will be 100 roubles.

Grammar notes. Sdaje sę mi (it seems to me) is an impersonal expression with dative mi. Pytati (to ask, to question) is distinct from prositi (to request). Trohų (a little) is used adverbially; the nasal vowel reflects the Proto-Slavic ǫ preserved in Interslavic orthography. The imperative dajte and ukažite use the standard second-person plural polite form.

Ostrižeńje​

Topic: At the hairdresser; appearance and colour

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Ostrižeńje
AZdråvo! Hòtěla byh sę ostrići.
BSadite sę, prošų. Kakòvų pričeskų vy želate?
AJa želala byh pričeskų kako děvčina na tom foto. Ględajte, ima grivkų i kråtke vlåsy. Čto myslite, bųde li ta pričeska izględati na mně dobro?
BDa, mysljų tako. Imate ovalno lice i grivka bųde izględati u vas dobro. Ale jeste blondinka, a děvčina na foto je brunetka. Želate li farbovati vlåsy?
AŽelala byh farbovati v rudy. Čto myslite?
BMysljų, že rudy vam ne bųde dobro. Može byti, temno rudy. Kako o ovom časopisu. Podoba li sę vam tòj kolor?
AVeljmi originalny kolor! Sųglåšų sę. Večeraś mę moj mųž ne pozna.

English translation​

LineEnglish
A haircut
AHello! I would like to have my hair cut.
BPlease sit down. What kind of hairstyle would you like?
AI would like the hairstyle like the girl in that photo. Look, she has a fringe and short hair. What do you think — will that hairstyle look good on me?
BYes, I think so. You have an oval face and the fringe will look good on you. But you are a blonde, and the girl in the photo is a brunette. Would you like to colour your hair?
AI would like to dye it red. What do you think?
BI think red won't suit you. Perhaps dark red. Like in this magazine. Do you like that colour?
AA very original colour! I agree. This evening my husband won't recognise me.

Grammar notes. The conditional hòtěla byh (I would like) is feminine form, with the l-participle hòtěla agreeing with the female speaker. Večeraś (this evening, tonight) uses the suffix -aś indicating "today" (cf. dneś — today). Sųglåšati sę (to agree) is a reflexive verb.

Moj rodinny album​

Topic: Family; physical resemblance; future plans

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Moj rodinny album
AŽelaš viděti moj rodinny album?
BS radosťjų.
AOvo sųt moji roditelji. Tu sųt veljmi mlådi. Ovo je jih svaťba.
BIzględaš velje kako tvoja mati. Imate jednakòve oči i usměh.
AMysliš tako? Vsi srodniki mojego otca kažųt, že izględam velje kako on. Imamo jednakòvy nos. Ględaj, ovo je moja babica. Ovdě ima 25 lět.
BKakòva krasavica! Tęžko je věriti, že ty i ja bųdemo imati někògda tòž 70 lět.
ARaděje ne råzmysljam o tom.
BA kto je tòj privlěkateljny hlåpec?
AOvo je moj brat. On je morjak. Uniforma izględa dobro na njem, ale ne?
BZělo. I kdě je sejčas?
AOn plava po Srědizemskomu morju. Už skoro prijěde v odpusk. Imati bųdemo zabavų za pråzdnovańje jego prijězda. Prijdeš?
BBezsųmno!

English translation​

LineEnglish
My family album
AWould you like to see my family album?
BWith pleasure.
AThese are my parents. They are very young here. This is their wedding.
BYou look a lot like your mother. You have the same eyes and smile.
AYou think so? All my father's relatives say that I look a lot like him. We have the same nose. Look, this is my grandmother. She is 25 years old here.
BWhat a beauty! It is hard to believe that you and I will also be 70 years old someday.
AI would rather not think about that.
BAnd who is that attractive young man?
AThis is my brother. He is a sailor. The uniform looks good on him, doesn't it?
BVery much so. And where is he now?
AHe is sailing on the Mediterranean Sea. He will be coming home on leave soon. We will have a party to celebrate his arrival. Will you come?
BCertainly!

Grammar notes. Velje (a lot, much) is an adverb used in comparisons. Raděje (rather, preferably) is a comparative adverb from råd (glad, willing). Bezsųmno (certainly, without doubt) uses the prefix bez- (without) plus sųmněńje (doubt). The future compound imati bųdemo places the infinitive before the auxiliary.

Kakòvo strånno hobbi!​

Topic: Unusual hobbies; comparison and surprise

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Kakòvo strånno hobbi!
AImaš li hobbi?
BImam. Ljubim pleteńje i sambo.
ASambo? Ne je li slučajno něko bojevo uměńje.
BDa, da, to je russko bojevo uměńje.
AAle pleteńje i sambo sųt popòlno ine věći! Pleteńje je råzumlivo. Mnogo děvčęt ljubęt pleteńje. No sambo! Udiviteljno! I koliko dòlgo praktikuješ sambo?
BUže desęť lět. Počęla jesm praktikovati sambo, kògda jesm studovala v školě. Zato sųt sę hlåpci iz našego klassa vsegda bojali mę.
ADobro. A tvoje drugo hobbi?
BPleteńje je veljmi uspokojęća dějateljnosť. Moja babica mę je naučila. Kromě togo, to je sposob ščęditi pěnęzi. Ja pletų dlja sebe modne šali, šapky i svetry. Ględaj, jesm splela sama tòj sveter. Podoba li ti sę to?
AZělo je... originalny.
BDobro, i jestli ti sę to ne podoba, možeš povědati pravdų, ja tę ne bųdų biti!

English translation​

LineEnglish
What a strange hobby!
ADo you have a hobby?
BI do. I love knitting and sambo.
ASambo? Isn't that some kind of martial art?
BYes, yes, it is a Russian martial art.
ABut knitting and sambo are completely different things! Knitting is understandable. Many girls love knitting. But sambo! Amazing! And how long have you been practising sambo?
BTen years already. I started practising sambo when I was studying at school. That is why the boys in our class were always afraid of me.
AI see. And your other hobby?
BKnitting is a very calming activity. My grandmother taught me. Besides, it is a way to save money. I knit fashionable scarves, hats and sweaters for myself. Look, I knitted this sweater myself. Do you like it?
AIt is very... original.
BFine, and if you don't like it, you can tell the truth — I won't hit you!

Grammar notes. Bojevo uměńje (martial art; literally "combat skill") is a common compound. Děvčę (girl) belongs to the neuter -ę stem declension; its genitive plural děvčęt shows the characteristic zero ending with vowel lengthening. Ščęditi pěnęzi (to save money) uses the accusative plural pěnęzi from the i-stem plural pěnęzi (money). Uspokojęća is a present active participle (calming).

Velika prośba​

Topic: Asking a favour; describing people's appearance

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Velika prośba
ALeno, imam k tobě velikų prośbų! Možeš li večeraś strětnųti mojų prijateljkų v aeroportu? Nemam vrěmena.
BRåzuměje sę. Ne je problem. Kako ona izględa?
AOna je privlěkateljna blondinka s zelenymi očami. Ona ne je vysoka. Ljubi nositi sportivnų odeđų. Bųde imati jarko oranževy kufer.
BImam nadejų, že ne bųde nekoliko blondinok s oranževymi kuframi.
ANe trevoži sę, ja opišų jej tvoj izględ.
BPovědaj jej, že ja bųdų v koričnevoj kurtkě i černyh džinsah.
AJej bųde dostatòčno znati, že jesi skoro dva metra vysoka.
BImaš pravdų, tęžko je mę ne primětiti.

English translation​

LineEnglish
A big favour
ALena, I have a big favour to ask you! Can you meet my friend at the airport this evening? I don't have the time.
BOf course. No problem. What does she look like?
AShe is an attractive blonde with green eyes. She is not tall. She likes wearing sportswear. She will have a bright orange suitcase.
BI hope there won't be several blondes with orange suitcases.
ADon't worry, I'll describe your appearance to her.
BTell her that I'll be wearing a brown jacket and black jeans.
AIt will be enough for her to know that you are nearly two metres tall.
BYou are right, it is hard not to notice me.

Grammar notes. Imam k tobě prośbų (I have a request of you) uses the prepositional phrase k + dative for the person addressed in a request. Skoro (almost, nearly) in this context is an approximative adverb. Tęžko je (it is difficult) is an impersonal construction with infinitive complement. Instrumental plural in clothing: v koričnevoj kurtkě i černyh džinsah uses locative for "in/wearing".

Kamo pojědem v odpusk?​

Topic: Planning a holiday; disagreement and compromise

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Kamo pojědem v odpusk?
AJa ne hòćų jěhati v Egipt. Jězdili jesmo minųlym godom i mi ne je spodobalo sę.
BA mi je spodobalo sę. Hòćų snova onamo pojěhati.
ATeplo morje, odlično nyrjańje.
BAle tam je prěmnogo goręće v sej sezon. Može, pojědemo v bolje hlådno město.
ANa Sěverny polus?
BNe rěči bezsmyslice! Pojědimo v Evropų, na priměr, v Rim. Možemo posětiti toliko kulturnyh měst. Poznamo toliko zanimlivogo.
AJa ne govorjų italijansky.
BArabsky ty takože ne govoriš, a hòćeš pojěhati v Egipt!
ATo ne je tòjže stvår...
BJědų v Rim i točka.
ANu, dobro... Ja takože.

English translation​

LineEnglish
Where shall we go on holiday?
AI don't want to go to Egypt. We went last year and I didn't enjoy it.
BI enjoyed it. I want to go there again.
AWarm sea, excellent diving.
BBut it is far too hot there at this time of year. Perhaps we should go somewhere cooler.
ATo the North Pole?
BDon't talk nonsense! Let's go to Europe, for example to Rome. We could visit so many cultural sites. We would learn so much interesting stuff.
AI don't speak Italian.
BYou don't speak Arabic either, yet you want to go to Egypt!
AThat is not the same thing...
BI am going to Rome and that's that.
AWell, fine... Me too.

Grammar notes. The directional kamo (where to, whither) contrasts with kdě (where, at what place). Prěmnogo (far too much, excessively) uses the prefix prě- (over-, too). Tòjže stvår (the same thing) employs the identity particle -že attached to the demonstrative. I točka (and that is that; and full stop) is a colloquial emphatic ending.

Nenaviđų domovų råbotų!​

Topic: Household chores; narrating past events; in-laws

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Nenaviđų domovų råbotų!
AMašo, pozdråv! Kako provela jesi svoj vikend?
BRaděje ne pytaj.
AZačto? Čto sę je slučilo?
BVse je započęla v sobotų. Izpŕva jesm počistila v dnevnom pokoju i izmyla vśų posųdų. Potom jesm oprala vśų odeđų i očistila koverec. I potom jesm izmyla okna.
AOh! Tako trudoljubiva jesi!
BPočkaj, ješče jesm ne zakončila! Potom takože jesm izmyla podlogų. Poslě sego jesm šla na zakupy. Konečno, jesm ogladila odeđų i začęla gotoviti večerjų.
AA mųž?
BA mųž je pomogal děťam s domašnjim zadańjem. Do nedělje jesmo musěli zavŕšiti vse zadaće i izglęđati kako idealno semejstvo.
AAle začto?
BV neděljų mę je svekròv posětila.
ADobro. Pa, i kako je bylo vse?
BOna je primětila pråh v garderobě i kazala, že povinna jesm čistiti čęšće.

English translation​

LineEnglish
I hate housework!
AMasha, hi! How did you spend your weekend?
BBetter not ask.
AWhy? What happened?
BIt all started on Saturday. First I cleaned the living room and washed all the dishes. Then I washed all the laundry and vacuumed the carpet. And then I washed the windows.
AOh! You are so hardworking!
BWait, I haven't finished yet! Then I also washed the floor. After that I went shopping. Finally, I ironed the laundry and started cooking dinner.
AAnd your husband?
BMy husband helped the children with their homework. By Sunday we had to finish all the chores and appear to be an ideal family.
ABut why?
BOn Sunday my mother-in-law came to visit.
AI see. Well, and how did it all go?
BShe noticed dust in the wardrobe and said that I should clean more often.

Grammar notes. Izpŕva (first of all, to begin with) is an adverb built from iz- + pŕv- (first). Musěti (must, to be obliged) is a modal verb. Svekròv (mother-in-law, husband's mother) is a consonant-stem noun. The dialogue also shows an alternative version in the source using simple past (aorist/imperfect) forms (počistih, izmyh), illustrating register variation between the compound past and the simple past.

Anja ima novogo prijatelja​

Topic: Gossip about relationships; character judgements

Interslavic​

LineInterslavic
Anja ima novogo prijatelja
AZnaš li, že Anja ima novogo prijatelja?
BOh! Je li lěpy?
ANe je moj vkųs, no on je veljmi umny i zanimlivy. On zna mnogo směšnyh pritč i veljmi prijazny.
BTo znači, že on je lěpši, než jej poslědnji prijatelj. Byl on je někako čudny. Nikògda sę ne je usměhal i ne jesmo imali obćih tem za råzgovor.
AJesi prava, mi je sę takože ne podobal. Ne ljubjų pessimistov. Kako Anja je mogla hoditi s njim...?
BKto vě, može byti, je imal někы skryty talent...
APravdopodobno. Ili prosto ona ljubila, čto on je tako bogaty i vsegda daril jej dråge podarky.
BA kto ne ljubi dråge podarky?
ATočno. A novy prijatelj ješče studuje. Bųde učiteljem biologije.
BUžas, učitelji segda zaråbotyvajųt duže malo...
AOh, ględaj! Anja ide!
BČao, Anjo! Kakòv surpriz! Govorimo o tom i onom, o politikě, o modě... a kamo ideš?

English translation​

LineEnglish
Anya has a new boyfriend
ADo you know that Anya has a new boyfriend?
BOh! Is he good-looking?
ANot my type, but he is very intelligent and interesting. He knows many funny stories and is very friendly.
BThat means he is better than her last boyfriend. He was somehow strange. He never smiled and we had no common topics to talk about.
AYou are right, I didn't like him either. I don't like pessimists. How could Anya go out with him...?
BWho knows, maybe he had some hidden talent...
AProbably. Or she simply liked that he was so rich and always gave her expensive presents.
BAnd who doesn't like expensive presents?
AExactly. And the new boyfriend is still studying. He will be a biology teacher.
BHow awful, teachers always earn so little...
AOh, look! Anya is coming!
BHi, Anya! What a surprise! We're talking about this and that, about politics, about fashion... and where are you going?

Grammar notes. Hoditi s njim (to go out with him, to date him) is an idiomatic use of hoditi (to go, to walk). Pritča (story, tale; parable) is a common Slavic literary word. Russky medvěď​

Topic: Facts about Russia; myth vs. reality; prose monologue with joke

Some foreigners think that bears walk along the streets in Russia. Of course, that is not true! Bears live in forests and do not like people.

An encounter with a bear can be very dangerous. Russian people love going into the forest to pick mushrooms and berries. They do it carefully, because bears also love berries and mushrooms and may attack a person. A bear eats everything: berries, fish, meat, and even insects. He especially loves honey.

In winter bears usually sleep, because the weather is too cold and there is no food. While the bear sleeps, it can lose 80 kilograms in weight! Russian people look very much like bears. In winter they also become slow and sleep a lot, because outside it is far too cold and dark.

That is why Russians love bears and call them "mishki". Misha was the mascot of the Summer Olympics of 1980 in Moscow.

Bear joke: Cold winter. A bear wanders through the forest, stops from time to time, bangs his head against a tree and cries: "Why, oh why did I drink so much coffee?!"

Interslavic​

Někòtori ćuđezemci mnęt, že v Rusiji medvědi hodęt po ulicah. Råzuměje sę, to ne je pravda! Medvědi žijųt v lěsah i ne ljubęt ljudij.

Strěća s medvědem može byti duže opasna. Russki ljudi ljubęt hoditi v lěs i sòbirati griby i jagody. Oni dělajųt to s vnimańjem, ibo medvědi takože ljubęt jagody i griby i mogųt napasti na člověka. Medvěď jěst vse: jagody, rybų, męso i daže insektov. Osoblivo on ljubi med.

V zimų medvědi obyčno spęt, ibo vrěmę je prěmnogo hlådno i nema jědy. Dokolě medvěď spi, on može shudnųti na 80 kilogrammov! Russki ljudi duže izględajųt kako medvědi. V zimų oni takože stajųt medli i mnogo spęt, ibo vo vně je prěmnogo hlådno i temno.

Zato že rusi ljubęt medvědev i zòvųt jih «miški». Miša je talisman lětnjej olimpiady 1980 goda v Moskvě.

Směška o medvědi: Hlådna zima. Medvěď blųka po lěsu, poněkda sę zaustavja, udarja glåvojų po drěvesu i kriči: «Nu, začto, začto jesm pil tako mnogo kavy?!»

Grammar notes. This text demonstrates continuous prose style. Dokolě (while, as long as) is a temporal conjunction. Shudnųti (to lose weight) uses the prefix s- for completion. Zòvųt jih «miški» (they call them "mishki") uses the accusative jih (them) as the direct object with the instrumental or nominative predicate in a naming construction. The joke uses the perfect jesm pil (I have drunk / I drank) with an exclamatory question.

Sankt-Peterburg​

Topic: History and culture of a city; descriptive prose

Interslavic​

Sankt-Peterburg je jedin iz najkrasivših grådov Rusije. On byl je osnovany imperatorom Petrom I (pŕvym). Imperator je rěšil izbudovati gråd sdě, da by odkryti dlja Rusije «okno v Evropų».

English translation​

Saint Petersburg is one of the most beautiful cities in Russia. It was founded by Emperor Peter I (the First). The Emperor decided to build a city here in order to open a "window onto Europe" for Russia.

Previously this place was a swamp, so building the city was very difficult. Many people died when they were building that city. That is why they say it is "a city built on bones".

In Saint Petersburg there are many beautiful palaces, museums and churches. The famous Amber Room is located here. In summer Saint Petersburg has "white nights".

Prividěńje​

Interslavic​

Jedno iz najznamenitějših prividěnij Sankt-Peterburga je prividěńje Pavla Pŕvogo. Ovo prividěńje žije v Mihajlovskom zamku, kdě žil i byl je ubijen imperator.

Raz podčas bala imperator naglo omdlěl. Kògda on je sę sbudil, to ględnųl s bezumnymi očami na vsih gostev i kazal: «Napravdų udušęt mę?» Kako rezultat, vse je sę i slučilo točno tako.

English translation​

One of the most famous ghosts of Saint Petersburg is the ghost of Paul the First. This ghost lives in the Mikhailovsky Castle, where the Emperor lived and was murdered.

Once during a ball, the Emperor suddenly fainted. When he came round, he looked at all the guests with wild eyes and said: "Will they really strangle me?" As a result, that is exactly what happened.

When the Emperor was 47 years old, he was strangled in his own castle. Several days before his death he said that he could see blood on the white walls of his bedroom. After the Emperor's death, no one lived in the castle for a long time. The city was full of terrifying stories about the ghost.

Now the Russian Museum is located in the Mikhailovsky Castle. The museum staff do not like staying there in the evening. They say that in the empty palace doors open and close by themselves and the floor creaks. The guards sometimes see the figure of a man holding a candle. When this happens, they say quietly to him: "Good night, Your Majesty."

Grammar notes. Omdlěti (to faint) and sbuditi sę (to come round, to wake up) are prefixed verbs of the body. Naglo (suddenly, unexpectedly) is the adverb from nagly. Udušati (to strangle, to smother) uses the prefix u- for completion. So svěćejų v rukě (with a candle in hand) uses the instrumental svěćejų and the locative v rukě. Vaše Veličenstvo (Your Majesty) is a fixed honorific form.

Naše selo — The Pan-Slavic Relay​

Topic: Village life; nostalgia; literary prose

This text originates from the Pan-Slavic Relay of September–October 2009, an event in which participants in various Slavic constructed languages translated a passage in relay fashion. The source passage was taken from a nineteenth-century English essay by Mary Russell Mitford. The Interslavic version below is by Jan van Steenbergen, representing the Slovianski stage of the relay; it is included here as a literary register sample distinct from the conversational dialogues above.

Interslavic — Slovianski​

Iz vsih možlivih ideal'nih mest, v ktorih živut ljudi, ja najviše-mnogo ljubim male selo, daleko od šumnogo groda, s jego maloju společnostju. Ono ne ima prepolnenih blokov, tol'ko male drevjane budinki – proste i malovatel'ne mesto dlja žitja, s žiteljami, ktorih obliča sut rovno znajome kak cveti v našem sade. To je zatvoreni svet s nemnogo ljudami, blizko sojedinenimi kak mrovki v mrovkovoj kope, pčeli v pčel'nike, ovci v ovečkom gumne, muhi v mušskom gnezde ili morniki na korable, gde vsekto znaje vsekogo i vsekto je znani od vsekogo, gde každi interesuje sja v každom i každi može imat' nadeju, čo nekto interesuje sja v njego.

Kak milo bilo bi zanurit' sja v to serdečne čuvstvo ljubenija i neznanih običajov, sobrat' sja i bit' prijateli s timi vsimi jedinstvenimi ljudjami okolo nas! Tak, čo mi budeme znat' i ljubit' vse kuti i obroti tenjovih ulic i solnečne pokrivne kanali, prez ktore mi prehodime celi den'.

Libokak, mala social'na grupa, ktora je sel'ska společnost', je to, čemu poezija i proza djakujut najviše-mnogo. Dolga, roztjagnuta droga, bludžuča sja v milem, teplom dne i prehodžuča veliku, široku drogu polnu avt i tirov. Vi ne hočete putovat' s mnoju, droge čitateli? Put' ne bude dolga. Mi počeme na konce dolini i odtud poideme v goru.

English translation (after Steeven Radzikowski)​

Of all possible ideal places for people to live, the one I love the most is a small village, far from the noise of the city, with its small community. It does not have crowded apartment blocks, only small wooden cottages — a simple and picturesque place to live, with inhabitants whose faces are as familiar as the flowers in our garden. It is an enclosed world with few people, closely united like ants in an ant-hill, bees in a hive, sheep in a sheep-fold, flies in a swarm, or sailors on a ship — where everyone knows everyone and everyone is known by everyone, where each person takes an interest in every other and each person may hope that someone takes an interest in him.

How pleasant it would be to immerse oneself in that warm feeling of love and unfamiliar customs, to gather together and be friends with all those unique people around us! So that we would come to know and love all the corners and turns of the shaded streets and sun-drenched meadows that we pass through every day.

In any case, a small social group — which is a village community — is what poetry and prose value most of all. A long, stretched-out road, winding its way through a mild, warm day and crossing a large wide road full of cars and lorries. Do you not want to travel with me, dear reader? The journey will not be long. We start at the end of the valley and from there we go uphill.

Grammar notes. This relay text represents an early stage of what became modern Interslavic (then called Slovianski). It uses -t' for the soft sign in infinitives and sja as the reflexive particle, reflecting the Slavic-normalised orthography of the period. Several lexical choices (libokak for "in any case", djakujut for "are grateful for", drogu polnu avt for "road full of cars") illustrate how literary and colloquial registers handled internationalisms. The passage also demonstrates complex relative clause nesting and the deliberate pastoral register that distinguishes literary Interslavic from the conversational dialogues in sections 1–19.
