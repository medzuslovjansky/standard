Motion vs. Static Alternations​

Several prepositions govern different cases depending on motion (direction) vs. static location:

PrepositionStatic (location)Motion (direction)
nalocative: na stolu "on the table"accusative: na stol "onto the table"
v/volocative: v domu "in the house"accusative: v dom "into the house"
podinstrumental: pod stolom "under the table"accusative: pod stol "under the table (motion)"
nadinstrumental: nad stolom "above the table"accusative: nad stol "up above the table (motion)"
prědinstrumental: prěd domom "in front of the house"genitive/accusative: prěd dom "(motion) in front of"
zainstrumental: za domom "behind the house"accusative: za dom "behind the house (motion)"
meže/medžuinstrumental: meže dvoma domami "between two houses"—

Selected Preposition Notes​

  • bez "without" always governs genitive: bez vody "without water"
  • do "to, until" always governs genitive: do Moskvy "to Moscow", do večera "until evening"
  • iz "out of" always governs genitive: iz doma "out of the house"
  • k/ko "to, toward" always governs dative: k mně "to me", ko dveri "to the door"
  • od "from" always governs genitive: od brata "from (a) brother"
  • pri "at, near, during" always governs locative: pri rěcě "at the river"
  • s/so + instrumental = "together with": s bratom "with (a) brother"
  • u "at, by" always governs genitive: u mene "at my place / I have"
