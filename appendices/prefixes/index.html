Appendix 4: Verbal Prefix Inventory

Prefixes attach primarily to verbs but may also modify nouns and adjectives. The base form of any prefixed verb is perfective; secondary imperfectives are derived from it.

Secondary Imperfective Formation from Prefixed Verbs​

| Perfective ending | Imperfective suffix | Example | |------------------|--------------------|---------|| | -ati | -yvati (hard) / -ivati (soft) | sodržati → sodrživati | | -iti | -jati (vowel of stem lengthened) | obnoviti → obnavjati | | -nuti | -ati | oddohnuti → oddyhati | | monosyllabic + vowel | -vati | prodati → prodavati |

Notes on Prefix Stacking and Interaction​

  • A single verb may carry two prefixes (e.g., prě- + u-, nad- + do-), though this is uncommon.
  • obez- is a fused prefix (ob- + bez-) and always derives verbs meaning deprivation.
  • nedo- is a fused prefix (ne- + do-) expressing incomplete or insufficient action.
  • Prefix ne- on adjectives/nouns is strictly negative (not a verbal prefix): nedobry "not good".
  • Prefix naj- on adjectives forms superlatives: najdobry "best" (not a verbal prefix).
  • South Slavic varieties use iz- where North/West Slavic uses vy-: both are valid in Interslavic.
