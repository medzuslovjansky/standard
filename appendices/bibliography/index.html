Bibliography and Normative References

info

This appendix lists the normative references on which the Interslavic specification rests, followed by a classified bibliography of descriptive sources for the individual Slavic languages and for comparative and historical Slavic linguistics. Entries are grouped thematically and ordered alphabetically by author within each group. Citation style follows a modified Chicago author–date convention.

Part I. Normative References​

The following sources have normative status for this specification. Any conflict between this document and a normative reference is resolved in favour of this specification as the primary standard; normative references are authoritative for factual claims about individual Slavic languages and for the reconstructed Proto-Slavic forms cited in morphological tables.

N1. Primary Interslavic Specification Materials​

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Grammar (online reference grammar). Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/grammar.html. Continuously updated; the version current at the date of publication of this specification is considered normative for all grammatical paradigms.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Orthography. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/orthography.html. Normative for the standard Latin and Cyrillic alphabets and the etymological alphabet extension.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Phonology and Pronunciation. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/phonology.html. Normative for the phoneme inventory and pronunciation guidelines.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Vocabulary / Derivation. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/derivation.html. Normative for the word-formation system and morphophonological alternation rules.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Vocabulary / Word Formation. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/word_formation.html. Normative for derivational affixes and compounding rules.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Flavourisation. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/flavorizacija.html. Normative for the description of dialectal and register variation (flavorization) within Interslavic.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Design Criteria. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/design-criteria.html. Normative for the principles governing lexical selection and grammatical compromise.

Van Steenbergen, Jan, and Vojtěch Merunka. Medžuslovjansky jezyk (unified post-2017 standard). Committee document produced following the First Conference on the Interslavic Language (CISLa 2017), Staré Město, Czech Republic, June 2017. Normative for all post-merger standardisation decisions.

N2. Interslavic Lexical Resources​

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Dictionaries (multilingual). Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/slovniky.html. Normative word-list for lexical entries cited in this specification.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Interslavic Voting Machine (computational tool). Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/voting_machine.html. Implements the majority-voting algorithm described in the relevant section of this specification; results produced by this tool are considered authoritative for lexical derivation questions not otherwise resolved by the grammar.

Part II. Descriptive and Historical Sources​

Section A. Interslavic and Pan-Slavic Constructed Languages​

Bambas, Vaceslav. Vsjeslovianьskyь jazyk. Prague, 1861. Early pan-Slavic language project based on Church Slavonic; cited in the historical survey.

Herkeľ, Ján. Elementa universalis linguae Slavicae e vivis dialectis eruta et sanis logicae principiis suffulta. Budapest: Landerer, 1826. First systematic West-Slavic-based pan-Slavic grammar; basis for the entry Universalis Lingua Slavica in the historical overview.

Križanić, Juraj. Gramatično izkazanje ob russkom jeziku [grammatical exposition of the Russian language]. Written c. 1666; first published in part in: Bezsonov, Pyotr, ed. Русское государство в половине XVII века [The Russian State in the mid-seventeenth century]. Moscow, 1859–1860. The earliest documented constructed Interslavic language.

Majar-Ziljski, Matija. Uzajemni pravopis slavjanski [Mutual Slavic Orthography]. Zagreb, 1865. Introduced the comparative-grammar approach to pan-Slavic language design; directly anticipates the methodology of modern Interslavic.

Merunka, Vojtěch. Novoslověnsky jazyk [Neoslavonic]. Prague, 2010. Published grammar of the Novoslověnsky/Neoslavonic project; merged with Interslavic after CISLa 2017.

Merunka, Vojtěch, and Jan van Steenbergen. "Medžuslovjansky: A Proposal for a Modern Pan-Slavic Language." Slovjani.info, 2017. Available at: http://slovjani.info/. Summarises the post-merger unified standard.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. A Short History of Interslavic. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/history.html. Primary narrative source for (historical context) of this specification.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Language Comparison: Interslavic and Other Languages. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/faq.html. Source for the comparative discussion of Interslavic versus Russian, Esperanto, Slovio, and Old Church Slavonic.

Van Steenbergen, Jan. Introduction to Constructed Slavic Languages. Available at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/conlangs.html. Surveys the full tradition of pan-Slavic constructed languages from the 17th century to the present.

Zamenhof, Ludwik Łazarz. Fundamento de Esperanto. Paris: Hachette, 1905. Included for comparative reference; Esperanto's design principles are contrasted with those of Interslavic in the relevant section

Section B. Old Church Slavonic and Proto-Slavic​

Diels, Paul. Altkirchenslavische Grammatik. 2 vols. Heidelberg: Winter, 1932–1934. Standard reference grammar of Old Church Slavonic used for canonical paradigm reconstruction.

Lunt, Horace Gray. Old Church Slavonic Grammar. 7th revised ed. Berlin: Mouton de Gruyter, 2001. The standard English-language reference grammar of OCS; used for paradigms cited in the historical morphology sections of this specification.

Mayer, Gerald L. "The Proto-Slavic Vowel System." In: Papers in Slavic Phonology, edited by H. Baran. Ann Arbor: Michigan Slavic Publications, 1976. Background source for vowel reconstruction principles.

Meillet, Antoine. Le slave commun. 2nd ed. Paris: Champion, 1934. Foundational comparative grammar of Common Slavic (Proto-Slavic); primary reference for reconstructed proto-forms cited in this specification.

Shevelov, George Y. A Prehistory of Slavic: The Historical Phonology of Common Slavic. Heidelberg: Winter, 1964. Detailed treatment of Proto-Slavic phonological development; consulted for etymological alphabet correspondences.

Vaillant, André. Grammaire comparée des langues slaves. 5 vols. Paris: Klincksieck / Lyon: IAC, 1950–1977. Most comprehensive comparative grammar of Slavic; normative reference for reconstructed morphological paradigms.

Section C. Comparative Slavic Linguistics​

Carlton, Terence R. Introduction to the Phonological History of the Slavic Languages. Columbus, OH: Slavica, 1991. Accessible overview of Common Slavic phonological developments; used for vowel shift summaries.

Comrie, Bernard, and Greville Corbett, eds. The Slavonic Languages. London: Routledge, 1993. Comprehensive typological survey of all Slavic languages; primary reference for individual-language data used in the voting system.

Sussex, Roland, and Paul Cubberley. The Slavic Languages. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2006. Up-to-date reference for synchronic and diachronic properties of all Slavic languages; used throughout for language-specific grammatical and lexical data.

Section D. Individual Slavic Languages​

The following grammars and dictionaries were consulted for the language-specific data that feeds the Interslavic voting system and for the false-friend pairs documented in Appendix 10.7.

Bulgarian​

Scatton, Ernest A. A Reference Grammar of Modern Bulgarian. Columbus, OH: Slavica, 1984.

Czech​

Naughton, James. Czech: An Essential Grammar. London: Routledge, 2005.

Short, David. "Czech." In: Comrie and Corbett 1993, 455–532.

Polish​

Swan, Oscar E. A Grammar of Contemporary Polish. Bloomington: Slavica, 2002.

Bielec, Dana. Polish: An Essential Grammar. London: Routledge, 1998.

Russian​

Wade, Terence. A Comprehensive Russian Grammar. 3rd ed. Oxford: Wiley-Blackwell, 2010.

Cubberley, Paul. "Russian." In: Comrie and Corbett 1993, 324–395.

Serbo-Croatian / Bosnian / Croatian / Serbian​

Alexander, Ronelle. Bosnian, Croatian, Serbian: A Grammar with Sociolinguistic Commentary. Madison: University of Wisconsin Press, 2006.

Browne, Wayles, and Alt Alt. "Serbo-Croat." In: Comrie and Corbett 1993, 306–387.

Slovak​

Naughton, James. "Slovak." In: Comrie and Corbett 1993, 533–592.

Slovene​

Priestly, Tom M. S. "Slovene." In: Comrie and Corbett 1993, 388–451.

Ukrainian​

Shevelov, George Y. A Historical Phonology of the Ukrainian Language. Heidelberg: Winter, 1979.

Pugh, Stefan M., and Ian Press. Ukrainian: A Comprehensive Grammar. London: Routledge, 1999.

Section E. Lexicographic and False-Friend Resources​

Wiktionary contributors. False Friends of the Slavist. English Wikibooks. Available at: http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/False_Friends_of_the_Slavist. Ongoing collaborative list of inter-Slavic false cognates; cited in the design criteria documentation and used as a starting point for Appendix 10.7.

Zaliznyak, Andrey A. Grammatičeskij slovar' russkogo jazyka [Grammatical Dictionary of the Russian Language]. 6th ed. Moscow: AST-Press, 2008. Used for inflectional class data in Russian.

Section F. Linguistic Theory and Methodology​

Auger, Julie, and Jeffrey Steele. "Structure of a Constructed Language." In: Oxford Handbook of the History of Linguistics, edited by Keith Allan. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2013. Background on the typology of constructed auxiliary languages.

Blanke, Detlev. Internationale Plansprachen: Eine Einführung. Berlin: Akademie-Verlag, 1985. Standard theoretical survey of planned/constructed languages; provides the analytic framework for comparing Interslavic with Esperanto and other auxlangs.

Gröschel, Bernhard. Das Serbokroatische zwischen Linguistik und Politik. Munich: Lincom, 2009. Referenced for the discussion of Bosnian/Croatian/Serbian standardisation.

Matthews, Peter H. Oxford Concise Dictionary of Linguistics. 3rd ed. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2014. Terminological reference for all linguistic technical terms defined in Appendix 10.6.

Part III. Online and Community Resources​

Interslavic Language Portal. https://interslavic.online/. The primary public-facing home of the Interslavic standard, housing the grammar, dictionary, and learning materials.

Interslavic Wikipedia. https://isv.wikipedia.org/. Collaborative encyclopaedia written in Interslavic; an ongoing corpus of authentic Interslavic text used for style reference.

Interslavic Facebook Group. https://www.facebook.com/groups/interslavic. Principal community forum; referenced for information about community size and language testing events.

CISLa 2017 — First Conference on the Interslavic Language. Proceedings and programme archived at: http://cisla.slavic-union.org/. Historical record of the conference at which the modern unified Interslavic standard was agreed.

Slovjani.info. http://slovjani.info/. Scientific journal publishing articles in or with abstracts in Interslavic; established 2017.

The Painted Bird (film). Dir. Václav Marhoul. Czech Republic, 2019. Feature film whose dialogue was written in Interslavic; the first large-scale public deployment of Interslavic in a mass-media context. Information at: http://steen.free.fr/interslavic/the_painted_bird.html.
