Appendix 1: Noun Declension Reference Tables

All paradigms shown in both scripts. Hard stems listed first; soft-stem variants in parentheses where they differ.

Declension 1 — Masculine (hard/soft)​

Examples: brat "brother" (animate), dom "house" (inanimate), muž "man" (soft, animate), kraj "country" (soft, inanimate)

Casebrat (sg)brat (pl)dom (sg)dom (pl)muž (sg)muž (pl)kraj (sg)kraj (pl)
Nombratbratidomdomymužmužikrajkraje
Accbratabratovdomdomymužamuževkrajkraje
Genbratabratovdomadomovmužamuževkrajakrajev
Datbratubratamdomudomammužumužamkrajukrajam
Insbratombratamidomomdomamimužemmužamikrajemkrajami
Locbratu/bratěbratahdomu/domědomahmužu/mužimužahkraju/krajikrajah
Vocbratebratidomedomymužumužikrajukraje

Notes:

  • Animate acc.sg. = gen.sg.; inanimate acc.sg. = nom.sg.
  • Animate nom.pl. ends in -i; inanimate in -y (soft: -e)
  • After soft consonants: -om→-em, -ov→-ev, -y→-e
  • Loc.sg. preferred -u; -ě (hard) / -i (soft) also accepted
  • Words in -anin drop -in- in plural: Slovjanin → Slovjani, Slovjanov

Declension 1 — Neuter (hard/soft)​

Examples: slovo "word", morje "sea" (soft), ime "name" (athematic -men-)

Caseslovo (sg)slovo (pl)morje (sg)morje (pl)ime (sg)ime (pl)
Nomslovoslovamorjemorjaimeimena
Accslovoslovamorjemorjaimeimena
Genslovaslovmorjamorejimenaimen

Insslovomslovamimorjemmorjamiimenemimenami
Locslovu/slověslovahmorju/morjimorjahimenuimenah

Notes:

  • Nom./acc./voc. always identical
  • Gen.pl. zero ending -Ø; epenthetic -o- (hard: okno→okon) or -e- (soft/before j: morje→morej)
  • -men- stem nouns (ime, rame, brěme): gen.sg. -ena, dat.sg. -enu
  • -et- stem nouns (tele "calf", prase "piglet"): gen.sg. -eta, dat.sg. -etu

Notes:

  • Hard: -y / -ě / -u / -oju → Soft: -e / -i / -u / -eju
  • Gen.pl. zero ending; epenthetic vowel inserted if needed
  • Masculine nouns in -a (sluga, sudja) follow this declension in sg., masculine animate plural in pl.
