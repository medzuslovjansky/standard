Adverbs

This section specifies the formation, comparison, and use of adverbs in Interslavic. All normative rules use the terms MUST, SHALL, SHOULD, and MAY as defined in RFC 2119.

Formation from Adjectives​

Adverbs in Interslavic are formed from adjectives by replacing the adjectival ending with the suffix -o (after hard stems) or -e (after soft stems — i.e., stems ending in š, ž, č, or j). The resulting form is identical to the neuter singular nominative/accusative of the corresponding adjective.

AdjectiveAdverbGloss
dobrydobro"well"
prostyprosto"simply"
tihytiho"quietly"
svěžisvěže"freshly"
gorjačigorjače"hotly"

The short form of adjectives MUST NOT be confused with the corresponding adverb. The adverb is always identical to the neuter singular form of the long adjective.

Comparative and Superlative of Adverbs​

Synthetic Comparative Adverb (-ěje / -eje)​

Comparative adverbs are derived from the synthetic adjective comparative by replacing the comparative suffix -ši with -e, yielding the ending -ěje (after hard stems) or -eje (after soft stems):

Positive adverbComparative adverbGloss
novonověje"more newly"
čistočistěje"more cleanly"
tihotišeje"more quietly"
svěžesvěžeje"more freshly"

For adjectives (and their derived adverbs) whose stem ends in -ky, -eky, or -oky, the comparative adverb is formed by adding -je directly to the root, triggering iotation of the preceding consonant (x):

Positive adverbComparative adverbGloss
dalekodalje"farther"
širokoširje"more broadly"
vysokovyše"higher"
blizkobliže"closer"
rědkorědže"more rarely"

Analytic Comparative Adverb (vyše / bolje + positive)​

The analytic comparative of adverbs is formed by placing vyše or bolje ("more") before the positive adverb. This construction is equivalent to the synthetic form in meaning:

  • vyše prosto — "more simply"
  • bolje tiho — "more quietly"
Unverified Content

The source describes the analytic form as "most likely encountered" with long/foreign words and participles — an observation of tendency, not a normative SHOULD recommendation. The MAY clause for common adverbs is also editorial.

The analytic form SHOULD be preferred for long words, words of foreign origin, and adverbs derived from participles. Either form MAY be used for common adverbs.

Superlative Adverb (naj- prefix)​

The superlative of adverbs is formed by prefixing naj- to the comparative adverb:

PositiveComparativeSuperlativeGloss
prostoprostějenajprostěje"most simply"
novonovějenajnověje"most newly"
dalekodaljenajdalje"farthest"

The prefix naj- MAY also be applied to the analytic comparative:

  • najvyše prosto — "most simply"
  • najbolje tiho — "most quietly"

As a simplification, naj- MAY be prefixed directly to the positive adverb:

  • najnovo — "most newly"
  • najprosto — "most simply"

Less-Than Comparative and Least Superlative​

The adverb menje ("less") functions as the analytic marker for the less-than comparative:

  • menje prosto — "less simply"
  • menje tiho — "less quietly"

The adverb najmenje ("least") forms the least superlative:

  • najmenje prosto — "least simply"

Additional Degree Markers​

Excessive Degree (prě-)​

The prefix prě- attached to the positive form of the adjective (from which the adverb is derived) expresses an extreme or excessive degree ("very", "too", "overly"). An adverb derived from such a prefixed adjective carries the same meaning:

  • prěprosty → prěprosto — "too simply, overly simply"

Negative (ne-)​

Unverified Content

The claim that :isv[ne-] is "most commonly encountered in fixed lexical items" has no basis in the source material, which simply states that :isv[ne-] creates a negative without restricting its usage scope.

The prefix ne- creates a negative adverb. It is most commonly encountered in fixed lexical items:

  • neprosty → neprosto — "not simply, in a non-simple manner"
