Lexical Flavorization

Lexical flavorization selects among documented Interslavic synonyms according to their regional distribution. Unlike phonological or morphological flavorization, which alters the form of words, lexical flavorization chooses between distinct lexical items — each independently attested in the Slavic languages — whose geographic distribution differs across the Slavic dialect continuum.

All lexical choices specified in this chapter draw exclusively from the documented Interslavic dictionary. Introducing words borrowed solely from a single national language without broader Slavic etymological grounding does not constitute flavorization; it constitutes code-switching .

Regional Synonyms and Their Distribution​

Unverified Content

The formal labeling system (North/South/East/West) with the specific definitions in the table below is not described in the source material. The source mentions that the dictionary "sometimes offers synonyms, with one word covering one part of Slavic territory and a second word the other," but does not define a formal label taxonomy.

The Interslavic dictionary marks many synonyms with a regional preference label indicating which part of the Slavic territory the word is primarily associated with. The labels used are:

LabelMeaning
NorthPrimarily attested in North Slavic (Russian, Belarusian, Polish, Sorbian)
SouthPrimarily attested in South Slavic (Serbian, Croatian, Bosnian, Slovene, Bulgarian, Macedonian)
EastPrimarily attested in East Slavic (Russian, Ukrainian, Belarusian)
WestPrimarily attested in West Slavic (Polish, Czech, Slovak, Sorbian)

A synonym marked North is not incorrect in Southern contexts; it is simply less likely to be recognized immediately by a South Slavic audience. The principle governing lexical flavorization is therefore: for a given target audience, prefer the synonym whose regional distribution best overlaps with that audience.

Verbal Prefixes as Lexical Markers​

The prefix alternation vy- / iz- (documented morphologically in the relevant section) also has a lexical dimension: many prefixed verbs are lexicalized with one prefix but not the other, creating Northern and Southern vocabulary items.

Worked Lexical Flavorization Example​

The following parallel texts illustrate lexical flavorization. The same propositional content is expressed in standard Interslavic, then in the Northern canonical model, then in the Southern canonical model. Lexically flavorized items are marked with square brackets and annotated below.

Standard Interslavic:

Člověk, ktory žije v velikom gorode, ne vsegda imaje čas dlja čitanja knih.

Northern lexical flavorization:

Člověk, ktory žije v velikom gorode, ne vsegda imaje čas dlja čitanja knih.

Lexical choices (Northern):

  • ktory [Northern relative pronoun; Southern alternative: koj]
  • gorod [Northern word for city; Southern alternative: grad]
  • vsegda [Northern word for always; Southern alternative: uvěk]
  • dlja [Northern purposive preposition; Southern alternative: za]

Southern lexical flavorization:

Člověk, koj žije v velikom gradu, ne uvěk imaje čas za čitanje knih.

Lexical choices (Southern):

  • koj [Southern relative pronoun; Northern alternative: ktory]
  • grad [Southern word for city; Northern alternative: gorod]
  • uvěk [Southern word for always; Northern alternative: vsegda]
  • za [Southern purposive preposition; Northern alternative: dlja]

Observation: The Northern and Southern versions are mutually intelligible: all four substituted words are attested in the standard Interslavic dictionary, and any speaker of standard Interslavic can recover the meaning without difficulty. This illustrates the intelligibility threshold requirement of the relevant section
