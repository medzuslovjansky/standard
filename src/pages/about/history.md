# History of the Interslavic Language

## Origins

The Interslavic language project originated in **summer 2006**, established by contributors from West Slavic and South Slavic countries, plus participants from America and the Netherlands. The project explicitly maintains no political goals and avoids association with Pan-Slavic ideology.

## Key Milestones

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| **2006** | Project founded; contributors from multiple Slavic countries, America, and Netherlands |
| **2011** | Memorandum from the Interslavic Community published |
| **2017** | First international academic conference (CISLa); Committee established; unification of Slovianski and Novoslověnsky into a single standard completed |
| **2019** | Interslavic Manifesto published |
| **2024** | ISO 639-3 language code registration obtained (summer 2024) |
| **2025** | Interslavic Language Resolution published |

## Predecessor Projects

The modern Interslavic standard is the result of unifying two earlier variants:

- **Slovianski**
- **Novoslověnsky**

This unification was the founding task of the Committee, completed by summer 2017.

## Writing Systems

Interslavic uses three scripts:

- **Latin script** (primary)
- **Cyrillic script**
- **Glagolitic script** (historical/cultural use)
