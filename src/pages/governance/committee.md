# Committee of the Interslavic Language

The Committee is the governing body of the Interslavic language standard. It is independent of any single organization.

## Formation

Established in 2017 during the first international academic conference on the Interslavic language (CISLa). Its founding task — unifying two predecessor Interslavic variants (**Slovianski** and **Novoslověnsky**) into a single standard — was completed by summer 2017.

## Governance

The Committee operates through voting. Members are classified as:

- **Founding members** — involved since 2017
- **Ordinary members** — admitted after the founding period

## Key Documents

- **2011** — Memorandum from the Interslavic Community
- **2019** — Interslavic Manifesto
- **2025** — Interslavic Language Resolution (Latin ISV, Cyrillic ISV, English). Outlines social mission, programmatic objectives, ethical guidelines, and usage standards. Aims to "protect the Interslavic language from ideological abuse." Signed by five members representing Netherlands, Czechia, Ukraine, and Poland.
