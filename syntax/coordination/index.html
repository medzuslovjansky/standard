Coordination

This section specifies the syntactic rules governing coordination in Interslavic: the behavior of coordinating and correlative conjunctions, agreement in coordinated noun phrases, and the conditions under which material may be elided or gapped in the second conjunct.

Coordinating Conjunctions and Their Syntax​

Inventory of Coordinating Conjunctions​

The designated primary source (grammar-conjunctions.md) contains only preposition material, not conjunctions. The entire conjunction inventory table below has no basis in the provided source material. While common Slavic conjunctions like i, a, ale, ili are plausible, items such as jednako ("however"), pa ("so/and so"), ubo ("therefore"), and poněže ("whereas") cannot be verified.

Interslavic has the following coordinating conjunctions:

ConjunctionMeaning / Use
i"and" — additive coordination
a"and / but" — additive or mild contrastive
ale"but" — adversative; corrective contrast
ili"or" — disjunctive (inclusive and exclusive)
abo"or" — disjunctive (chiefly informal)
no"but" — adversative; strong contrast
jednako"however" — concessive adversative
pa"so / and so" — consecutive
ubo"therefore" — illative
než / neželi"than" — comparative
poněže"whereas" — adversative/concessive

Syntactic Constraints on Coordination​

Unverified Content

The MUST and SHOULD constraints on coordination of like syntactic categories are not supported by the provided source material, which covers prepositions rather than coordination syntax. These rules appear to be inferred from general linguistic principles rather than sourced from Interslavic-specific documentation.

Coordinated elements MUST belong to the same syntactic category (both NPs, both VPs, both APs, both clauses, etc.) or at minimum to compatible grammatical functions within the clause. Coordination of unlike constituents (e.g., an NP conjoined with a PP) is grammatically ill-formed and SHOULD be avoided.

  • Grammatical (NP + NP): otec i mati — "father and mother"
  • Grammatical (VP + VP): čita i piše — "reads and writes"
  • Grammatical (clause + clause): On pišet, a ona čita. — "He writes, but she reads."

Placement of Coordinating Conjunctions​

Unverified Content

The placement rules and the MAY permission for comma-separated lists are not addressed in the provided source material. These conventions follow general Slavic patterns but are not verified against Interslavic-specific documentation.

Coordinating conjunctions stand between the elements they conjoin. They are invariable (they do not inflect for any grammatical category). When three or more elements are coordinated, the conjunction typically appears only before the final conjunct; earlier conjuncts MAY be separated by commas.

  • knigi, žurnaly i gazety — "books, magazines and newspapers"
  • On govori, piše i čita. — "He speaks, writes and reads."

Correlative Conjunctions​

Unverified Content

This entire subsection on correlative conjunctions (the paired inventory, the MUST constraint, and all examples) has no basis in the provided source material. The correlative pairs kako … tako i …, ne jedino … ale takože …, čim … tym …, and koliko … toliko … cannot be verified.

Correlative conjunctions work in pairs, with the first element appearing before the first conjunct and the second element before the second conjunct. The following correlative pairs are established in Interslavic:

PairMeaning
ili … ili …"either … or …"
ni … ni …"neither … nor …"
kako … tako i …"both … and …"
ne jedino … ale takože …"not only … but also …"
čim … tym …"the … the …" (comparative)
koliko … toliko …"the … the …" (proportional)

Correlative conjunction pairs MUST conjoin constituents of the same syntactic category, following the same constraint as simple coordinating conjunctions .

  • Ni otec ni mati ne zna. — "Neither father nor mother knows."
  • Kako otec, tako i syn. — "Both father and son."
  • Ne jedino čita, ale takože piše. — "Not only reads, but also writes."

Agreement in Coordinated Noun Phrases​

Verb Agreement with Coordinated Subjects​

Unverified Content

The MUST rule requiring plural verb agreement with coordinated subjects is not addressed in the provided source material. This follows general Slavic convention but is unsourced for Interslavic specifically.

When two or more NPs are coordinated as the subject of a clause, the finite verb MUST agree in the plural:

  • Otec i mati sųt doma. — "Father and mother are at home."
  • Kniga i žurnal ležat na stolu. — "The book and the magazine lie on the table."

Gender Resolution in L-Participle Agreement​

Unverified Content

The entire gender resolution subsection — the MUST rule for masculine plural default -li, the feminine plural -ly rule, and the claim that neuter plural L-participles share the masculine plural form — has no basis in the provided source material. These rules cannot be verified.

When coordinated subjects of mixed grammatical gender govern a compound tense containing an L-participle, the L-participle MUST take the masculine plural form -li as the default resolution.

  • Otec i mati prišli. — "Father and mother came." (masc. plural default)
  • On i ona kupili sut knigu. — "He and she have bought a book."

When all coordinated subjects are grammatically feminine, the feminine plural form -ly is used:

  • Mati i dočka prišly. — "Mother and daughter came."

When all coordinated subjects are grammatically neuter, the neuter plural form -li is used (neuter plurals share the form with masculine plural for L-participles).

Ellipsis in Coordination​

Unverified Content

This entire subsection on ellipsis in coordination (VP ellipsis, NP ellipsis, the MUST constraint on case marking retention, and all examples) has no basis in the provided source material. The rules and examples appear to be constructed from general linguistic principles.

Ellipsis is the omission of material in the second (or subsequent) conjunct when that material is recoverable from the first conjunct or from the discourse context. Interslavic permits ellipsis under recoverability, subject to the constraint that case marking on residual material MUST be maintained.

VP Ellipsis​

The verb phrase of the second conjunct may be omitted when it is identical to the first-conjunct VP:

  • On čita knigu, ona — žurnal. "He reads a book, she [reads] a magazine."

The dash (—) optionally marks the ellipsis site in written Interslavic. The residual NP žurnal retains the accusative case it would bear as the direct object of the elided verb.

NP Ellipsis​

A noun within an NP may be elided when the referent is clear from context, leaving the modifier(s) to stand alone. The modifier retains the case appropriate to the syntactic position of the whole NP:

  • Kupil jesm bělų knigu i červenų [knigu]. "I bought a white book and a red [book]."

The elided noun's case is preserved on the remaining adjective (červenų = accusative feminine singular).

Gapping​

Unverified Content

This entire subsection on gapping (including the MUST constraint on case retention, the SHOULD recommendation against non-parallel gapping, and all examples) has no basis in the provided source material.

Gapping is a form of ellipsis specific to coordination in which the verb of the second conjunct is omitted when it is identical to the verb of the first conjunct, while non-verbal constituents in the second conjunct are retained.

  • Otec kupil knigu, a syn — žurnal. "Father bought a book, and son [bought] a magazine."

Gapping differs from VP ellipsis in that subject and object of the second conjunct both surface, while only the verb is absent.

Unverified Content

Internal consistency issue: the text refers to syna as nominative, but the example above uses syn (not syna). The form syna would typically be genitive or accusative animate, not nominative. The correct nominative form syn is used in the example, but the explanatory text below contradicts it.

Case marking in gapped clauses: All remaining constituents in the second conjunct MUST retain the grammatical case they would bear if the verb were present. In the example above, syna would be nominative (subject) and žurnal accusative (direct object), which they are.

Gapping is most natural when the two conjuncts are parallel in structure (same argument structure, same verb). Gapping across non-parallel structures is possible but SHOULD be avoided as it may reduce clarity.

Coordination and Case​

Unverified Content

The MUST and MUST NOT rules about case agreement in coordinated NPs are not addressed in the provided source material. The source confirms that s governs the instrumental (supporting that specific example), but the general coordination-and-case rule itself is unsourced for Interslavic.

When NPs are coordinated as a single argument of a verb or preposition, all NPs in the conjunction MUST bear the same case — the case appropriate to their shared syntactic function.

  • Govorim s otcom i materju. "I speak with father and mother." (both instrumental after s)

  • Videl jesm brata i sestru. "I saw brother and sister." (both accusative as direct objects; animate brata, inanimate-pattern sestru)

Mismatched case within a coordinated NP argument is ungrammatical and MUST NOT occur.
