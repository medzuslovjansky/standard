# Verbs (Community)

## Omission of `je` and `sųt` in Past Tense

When auxiliary **je** or **sųt** can be placed directly adjacent to the l-participle with no intervening words (e.g., "je došel" or "došel je"), the auxiliary is best to be omitted in the most cases.

### Exceptions

Maintain the auxiliary when:

1. Using intentionally the formal, official register
2. Writing in flowery or archaic style
3. When emphasizing that an action is complete and relevant to the present moment.
4. In questions (je li došel?) and any other syntax-sensitive constructs.

### Examples

**Natural omission:**

- "Včera on (je) prišel do doma v 20:00" 
- "Oni (sut) čitali knigu, a potom (sut) izošli do kafe"

Maintained for **"present perfect" emphasis** or **formality**:
- On je napisal rep-album, itak, on znaje něčto o rep-muzikě, ne li?
  - As opposed to: "On napisal svoj prvy rep-album v lětu 2007.
- Včera na samitu Evropy němečska delegacija je prědstavila svoju propoziciju o razvoju sektora nuklearnoj energetiky.

The goal is natural-feeling text that follows standard Slavic patterns while preserving meaning where needed.
