# 1.2. Fundamental Principles

## 1.2.1 Core Design Criteria

### 1.2.1.1 Maximum Mutual Intelligibility

Interslavic is crafted to be easily understood by speakers of all Slavic languages.

- **2.1.1.1 Selection of Words Common Across Slavic Languages**  
  Preference is given to vocabulary that is widely recognized among Slavic speakers.

- **2.1.1.2 Preference for Forms Understandable to the Majority**  
  Grammatical structures are chosen based on their familiarity and simplicity.

- **2.1.1.3 Avoidance of Region-Specific Idioms and Slang**  
  To maintain neutrality, colloquial expressions unique to certain regions are excluded.

### 1.2.1.2 Natural Development vs. Construction

While Interslavic is a constructed language, it strives to emulate the natural evolution of Slavic languages.

- **2.1.2.1 Emulation of Natural Language Evolution**  
  Incorporates historical developments and sound changes that are common in Slavic languages.

- **2.1.2.2 Incorporation of Widespread Linguistic Features**  
  Utilizes grammatical and phonological features prevalent across multiple Slavic languages.

- **2.1.2.3 Limitation of Artificial or Overly Constructed Elements**  
  Avoids introducing elements that lack basis in existing Slavic languages.

### 1.2.1.3 Simplicity and Regularity

Simplicity aids learning and usage.

- **2.1.3.1 Simplification of Complex Grammatical Rules**  
  Complex rules are streamlined to facilitate ease of learning.

- **2.1.3.2 Minimization of Exceptions and Irregular Forms**  
  Irregularities are minimized to promote predictability.

- **2.1.3.3 Consistent Application of Morphological Patterns**  
  Regular patterns are applied consistently across word classes.

### 1.2.1.4 Scalability

Interslavic accommodates users with varying proficiency levels.

- **2.1.4.1 Basic Level for Beginners**  
  Core vocabulary and essential grammar for new learners.

- **2.1.4.2 Intermediate and Advanced Levels for Proficient Users**  
  Additional complexity and nuance for advanced communication.

- **2.1.4.3 Flexibility to Accommodate Varying Levels of Fluency**  
  Resources and guidelines support continuous learning and mastery.

### 1.2.1.5 Cultural Neutrality

The language respects and unites diverse Slavic cultures.

- **2.1.5.1 Inclusive Approach to All Slavic Cultures**  
  No single Slavic language or culture is given preference.

- **2.1.5.2 Neutral Vocabulary Avoiding Cultural Bias**  
  Careful selection of terms to prevent cultural dominance.

- **2.1.5.3 Respect for Regional Diversity and Traditions**  
  Acknowledges and appreciates the richness of Slavic heritage.

## 1.2.2 Linguistic Basis

### 1.2.2.1 Proto-Slavic Foundations

Interslavic is deeply rooted in the Proto-Slavic language.

- **2.2.1.1 Common Phonological Features Inherited**  
  Preserves Proto-Slavic sounds that are still present in modern languages.

- **2.2.1.2 Morphological Structures from Proto-Slavic**  
  Utilizes ancient declension and conjugation patterns.

- **2.2.1.3 Shared Lexical Roots and Cognates**  
  Emphasizes words with common ancestry across Slavic languages.

### 1.2.2.2 Influence of Old Church Slavonic

Old Church Slavonic contributes to the historical depth of Interslavic.

- **2.2.2.1 Historical Orthographic Elements**  
  Incorporates letters like **Ě ě** and nasal vowels **Ę ę**, **Ų ų**.

- **2.2.2.2 Integration of Classical Vocabulary**  
  Includes words of significant cultural and historical importance.

- **2.2.2.3 Grammatical Constructs from Historical Texts**  
  Draws upon classical grammar for a richer linguistic structure.

### 1.2.2.3 Shared Features Among Modern Slavic Languages

- **2.2.3.1 Harmonization of Grammatical Categories**  
  Aligns cases, genders, and numbers for consistency.

- **2.2.3.2 Phonetic Correspondences and Sound Laws**  
  Recognizes patterns like palatalization and vowel changes.

- **2.2.3.3 Lexical Similarities and Standardization**  
  Standardizes vocabulary where possible, considering variations.

- **2.2.3.4 Strategies for Resolving Linguistic Discrepancies**  
  Provides guidelines for choosing forms when languages differ.
