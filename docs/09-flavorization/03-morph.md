# 9.3. Morphological Flavorization

Morphological flavorization selects among documented inflectional and derivational variants of Interslavic to increase familiarity for speakers of a particular dialect area.
Unlike phonological flavorization, which operates on sounds, morphological flavorization operates on grammatical forms: endings, pronouns, prefixes, and prepositions.

All morphological variants specified in this chapter are **optional**.
Standard Interslavic forms are always acceptable regardless of target audience.
:::caution[Unverified Content]
The MUST requirement for intelligibility and the cross-reference to §9.1.3 are not stated in the source material. The source discusses intelligibility as a general goal but does not formalize it as a normative constraint with a section reference.
:::

A morphologically flavorized text MUST remain fully intelligible to any speaker of standard Interslavic (§9.1.3).

---

## 9.3.1. Infinitive Ending: `-ti` vs. `-ť`

Standard Interslavic uses the infinitive ending :isv[-ti], which is the form found across South Slavic, Czech, Slovak, and Ukrainian.

**Rule for Northern flavor:** The infinitive ending SHOULD be shortened to :isv[-ť].
:::caution[Unverified Content]
The characterization of Belarusian as `-ць` and the claim about Polish/Sorbian "tendency toward reduced endings" are not in the source material. The source simply states that Northern infinitives end in `-ť`.
:::

This ending reflects the East Slavic pattern (Russian _-ть_, Belarusian _-ць_) and aligns with the Polish/Sorbian tendency toward reduced endings.

| Standard | Northern | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| :isv[byti] | :isv[byť] | to be |
| :isv[govoriti] | :isv[govoriť] | to speak |
:::caution[Unverified Content]
Internal inconsistency: the Northern form `dělaľ` does not follow the stated rule of replacing `-ti` with `-ť`. The expected form would be `dělať`.
:::

| :isv[dělati] | :isv[dělaľ] | to do |
| :isv[pisati] | :isv[pisať] | to write |
| :isv[znajti] | :isv[znajť] | to find |

The shortened ending applies uniformly to all infinitives regardless of conjugation class.
The internal stem, including any final consonant alternation, is unchanged.

| Flavor | Infinitive marker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (default) | :isv[-ti] | :isv[pisati] |
| Northern | :isv[-ť] | :isv[pisať] |

Regional reference:

| Polish | Ukrainian | Bulgarian | Czech | Serbian |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `-ć` / `-c` | `-ти` | `-` (no infinitive) | `-t` | `-ти` |

**Note:** Bulgarian and Macedonian speakers use analytical constructions rather than an infinitive (§9.3.7). The Southern model retains :isv[-ti].

## 9.3.2. Adjectival Nominative/Accusative Neuter Singular: `-o` vs. `-e`

Standard Interslavic uses the neuter singular adjective ending :isv[-o] (e.g., :isv[dobro]), which is the South Slavic dominant form.

**Rule for Northern flavor:** The neuter singular nominative/accusative adjectival ending SHOULD be changed to :isv[-e] (e.g., :isv[dobre]).

| Standard | Northern | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| :isv[dobro děte] | :isv[dobre detja] | a good child |
| :isv[novo slovo] | :isv[nove slovo] | a new word |
| :isv[staro město] | :isv[stare město] | an old city |

The ending change applies to all adjectival forms in the neuter nominative/accusative singular, including possessive, demonstrative, and interrogative pronouns used adjectivally.

Regional reference:

| Polish | Ukrainian | Bulgarian | Czech | Serbian |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `-e` | `-е` | `-о` | `-é` | `-о` |

## 9.3.3. Relative Pronoun: `ktory` (North) vs. `koj` (South)

Interslavic provides two relative pronouns:

- :isv[ktory] — primarily North Slavic in orientation (cf. Polish _który_, Russian _который_).
- :isv[koj] — primarily South Slavic in orientation (cf. Serbian/Croatian _koji_, Macedonian _кој_).

Both forms are standard and fully grammatical.
Their choice is the primary relative-pronoun marker of Northern vs. Southern flavor.

**Rule for Northern flavor:** :isv[ktory] SHOULD be preferred.
**Rule for Southern flavor:** :isv[koj] SHOULD be preferred.

| Context | Northern | Southern |
|---|---|---|
| Masc. sg. nom. | :isv[ktory] | :isv[koj] |
| Fem. sg. nom. | :isv[ktora] | :isv[koja] |
| Neut. sg. nom. | :isv[ktore] / :isv[ktoro] | :isv[koje] / :isv[kojo] |

Regional reference:

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The Bulgarian entry `який` appears incorrect — `який` is Ukrainian, not Bulgarian. Bulgarian uses `който` for the relative pronoun.
:::

| Polish | Ukrainian | Bulgarian | Czech | Serbian |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `który` | `котрий` | `який` | `který` | `који` |

## 9.3.4. Directional Prefix: `vy-` (North) vs. `iz-` (South)

Interslavic provides two competing prefixes expressing outward direction or origin:

- :isv[vy-] — North Slavic orientation (cf. Polish _wy-_, Russian _вы-_, Czech _vy-_).
- :isv[iz-] — South Slavic orientation (cf. Serbian/Croatian _iz-_, Slovene _iz-_).

**Rule for Northern flavor:** The prefix :isv[vy-] SHOULD be used.
**Rule for Southern flavor:** The prefix :isv[iz-] SHOULD be used.

| Meaning | Northern | Southern |
|---|---|---|
| to go out | :isv[vyjti] | :isv[izjti] |
| to speak out | :isv[vygovoriti] | :isv[izgovoriti] |
| to write out | :isv[vypisati] | :isv[izpisati] |
| to learn | :isv[vyučiti] | :isv[izučiti] |

Both prefixes may co-occur in the standard dictionary as synonyms with the same meaning; the regional marking governs which is preferred in a given flavor register.

Regional reference:

| Polish | Ukrainian | Bulgarian | Czech | Serbian |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `wy-` | `ви-` | `из-` | `vy-` | `из-` |

## 9.3.5. Purposive Preposition: `dlja` (North) vs. `za` (South) 'for'

The preposition 'for' with purposive meaning is expressed by two competing forms:

- :isv[dlja] — North Slavic orientation (cf. Russian _для_, Polish _dla_, Ukrainian _для_).
- :isv[za] — South Slavic orientation (cf. Serbian/Croatian _za_, Slovene _za_, Bulgarian _за_).

**Rule for Northern flavor:** :isv[dlja] SHOULD be used for purposive 'for'.
**Rule for Southern flavor:** :isv[za] SHOULD be used.

| Context | Northern | Southern | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| _for the people_ | :isv[dlja ljudej] | :isv[za ljudej] | — |
| _for this reason_ | :isv[dlja togo] | :isv[za to] | — |
| _for a long time_ | :isv[dlja dolgogo vremene] | :isv[za dolgo vrěme] | — |

**Note:** :isv[za] has other meanings in standard Interslavic (behind, after, during) that are unaffected by this flavorization rule.
Only the purposive 'for' sense is subject to Northern/Southern alternation.

Regional reference:

| Polish | Ukrainian | Bulgarian | Czech | Serbian |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `dla` | `для` | `за` | `pro` / `pro` | `за` |

## 9.3.7. Avoiding Infinitives and Cases: Bulgarian/Macedonian Flavor

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source material only briefly mentions "avoiding the use of infinitives and noun cases when addressing Bulgarians" as an example of grammatical-level flavourisation. The detailed rules in this section — the `da` + finite verb substitution patterns, the constraint on accusative retention, the prohibition on postposed articles, and the MUST/MUST NOT normative requirements — are elaborations not present in the source.
:::

Bulgarian and Macedonian have eliminated the inherited Slavic case system and the infinitive, replacing them with analytical constructions.
Standard Interslavic uses full case morphology and the infinitive; these are obligatory in the standard, and MUST be used unless a Bulgarian/Macedonian-specific accommodation is explicitly applied.

**Accommodation rule (Bulgarian/Macedonian flavor):**

1. **Infinitive:** Replace infinitive constructions with a finite verb introduced by the particle :isv[da] + present tense.

   | Standard | Bulgarian/Macedonian flavor | Gloss |
   |---|---|---|
   | :isv[Hoču pisati.] | :isv[Hoču da pišu.] | I want to write. |
   | :isv[Trěba govoriti.] | :isv[Trěba da govoriš.] | You need to speak. |
   | :isv[Mogu čitati.] | :isv[Mogu da čitam.] | I can read. |

2. **Case morphology:** Where context permits, analytical prepositional constructions MAY substitute for synthetic case endings.
   For the accusative (direct object), no preposition is available in Interslavic; the accusative form is retained even in Bulgarian/Macedonian flavor.

3. **Definiteness:** Bulgarian and Macedonian mark definiteness by a postposed article.
   Interslavic has no postposed article; this feature MUST NOT be introduced into flavorized Interslavic.
   Definiteness, where necessary, MAY be indicated by a demonstrative pronoun in prenominal position.

**Critical constraint:** This accommodation is intended strictly for texts directed at Bulgarian or Macedonian speakers who lack familiarity with case morphology.
It MUST NOT be applied as a general simplification in other contexts; doing so would reduce the morphological integrity of the text without communicative justification.

## 9.3.8. Worked Morphological Flavorization Examples

:::caution[Unverified Content]
These worked examples are not present in the source material. While the individual rules they demonstrate (infinitive shortening, prefix alternation, `dlja`/`za` substitution) are sourced, the specific sentences and especially the Bulgarian/Macedonian accommodation example (with `da piše`, `može da`) are original compositions that cannot be verified against the source.
:::

The following parallel texts illustrate morphological flavorization.
The base sentence is given in standard Interslavic; Northern and Southern variants follow with individual changes annotated.

**Base sentence (standard Interslavic):**

> :isv[Dobry člověk hoče pisati dlǎgu knigu, da by ju izučiti.]

---

**Northern morphological flavorization:**

> :isv[Dobry člověk hoče pisať dljagu knigu, da by ju vyučiť.]

Changes applied:
- Infinitive :isv[-ti] → :isv[-ť]: :isv[pisati] → :isv[pisať]; :isv[izučiti] → :isv[vyučiť]
- Prefix :isv[iz-] → :isv[vy-]: :isv[izučiti] → :isv[vyučiť]
- Preposition :isv[dlja] retained (Northern)

---

**Southern morphological flavorization:**

> :isv[Dobri člověk hoče pisati za dugu knigu, da by ju izučiti.]

Changes applied:
- Neuter/masc. adjectival ending: :isv[dobry] → :isv[dobri] (South Slavic preference)
- Preposition :isv[dlja] → :isv[za] (Southern)
- Prefix :isv[iz-] retained (Southern)
- Infinitive :isv[-ti] retained (Southern)

---

**Bulgarian/Macedonian accommodation:**

> :isv[Dobri člověk hoče da piše za duga kniga, da može da ja izuči.]

Changes applied:
- Infinitive replaced by :isv[da] + finite present: :isv[pisati] → :isv[da piše]
- Infinitive replaced by :isv[da] + finite present: :isv[izučiti] → :isv[da izuči]
- :isv[da by] modal replaced by :isv[može da] (analytic modal)
