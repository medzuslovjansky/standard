# A.10. Changelog and About This Specification

## About This Specification

The **Interslavic Language Standard Specification** is the normative reference document for *Medžuslovjansky jezyk* / *Меджусловјанскы језык* — a zonal auxiliary language constructed to be broadly intelligible to speakers of the Slavic language family without prior study.

This specification consolidates and supersedes the body of prior descriptive work on Interslavic. It is based primarily on Jan van Steenbergen's materials at steen.free.fr/interslavic, which represent the accumulated result of the *Slovianski* project (2006–2017) and its subsequent merger with *Novoslověnsky* (*Neoslavonic*) at CISLa 2017.

The specification covers:

- The full grammatical system including seven cases, three genders, verbal aspect, and full conjugation.

### Governance

Changes to normative content are managed by the **Interslavic Language Committee**, the successor body to the five-member unification committee formed in June 2017 following CISLa 2017. Proposed normative changes SHALL undergo:

1. Review against the twelve source languages ;
2. Assessment of impact on mutual intelligibility;
3. A public comment period of not less than thirty (30) days.

The version identifier follows the form `MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH` as specified in the relevant section

## Revision History

### Version 3.0 (current)

**Status:** Draft — under committee review

**Scope of changes:**

- Complete restructuring of the specification into ten normative sections plus appendices, superseding the earlier informal descriptive structure.
- Adoption of RFC 2119 normative keyword conventions (SHALL, MUST, SHOULD, MAY) throughout.
- Formal definition of the weighted six-subgroup voting mechanism  previously described only informally.
- Expanded phonological section  with IPA transcriptions and explicit phonotactic rules.
- Reorganized morphology chapter  with machine-readable paradigm tables.
- New sections on syntax , semantics and pragmatics , and flavorization .
- Version identifier scheme introduced .

**Known open items:** Sections marked `:::info[TODO: Committee Input Needed]` require formal committee decisions before this version is designated as a stable normative release. Sections marked `:::caution[Committee Review Needed]` contain unresolved source conflicts.

---

### Version 2.0

**Status:** Superseded

**Date:** 2017 — following CISLa 2017 (Staré Město, Czech Republic, 1–2 June 2017)

**Scope of changes:**

- Unification of *Slovianski* and *Novoslověnsky* into a single standard named *Medžuslovjansky*.
- Adoption of a unified orthography for both Latin and Cyrillic scripts.
- Harmonization of the morphological system drawing on both precursor projects.
- The name *Medžuslovjansky* formally adopted as the standard moniker, replacing *Slovianski*, *Medžuslovjanski*, and *Novoslověnsky*.
- Flavorization system formalized as the mechanism for regional adaptation, replacing ad-hoc variation.

**Principal authors:** Jan van Steenbergen, Vojtěch Merunka, and the CISLa 2017 unification committee.

---

### Version 1.x

**Status:** Superseded

**Date:** 2011–2017

**Scope of changes:**

- The name *Medžuslovjansky* (*Interslavic*) adopted in 2011 as the common moniker for converging projects, replacing *Slovianski*.
- Ongoing collaborative development on vocabulary, orthography, and grammar, coordinated via the Interslavic Language Portal and associated forums.
- Etymological (scientific) alphabet (*Naučny Medžuslovjansky*) described as an optional extension.

**Principal author:** Jan van Steenbergen, with contributions from the *Slovianski* community.

### Version 0.x — *Slovianski* Era

**Status:** Historical

**Date:** 2006–2011

**Summary:**

The *Slovianski* project was launched in 2006 as the first fully collaborative, internet-era inter-Slavic language effort. Its methodology — basing the language on systematic weighted comparison of twelve living Slavic languages rather than on any single model — established the core design principles that persist in the current specification.

In 2009, *Slovioski* (Radzikowski, Moraczewski, Borovička) was launched as a hybrid project; it merged into *Interslavic* in 2010. In 2010, *Novoslověnsky* (*Neoslavonic*, Merunka, Molhanec) launched as a parallel naturalistic project based on Old Church Slavonic modernization; the two projects collaborated increasingly closely until the 2017 unification.

### Historical Precursors (pre-2006)

This specification acknowledges the following historical projects as direct methodological precursors:

| Project | Author | Year | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| *Ruski* (manuscript) | Juraj Križanić | 1666 | First systematic inter-Slavic grammar |
| *Universalis Lingua Slavica* | Ján Herkeľ | 1826 | West Slavic comparative basis |
| *Uzajemni Pravopis Slavjanski* | Matija Majar-Ziljski | 1865 | Introduced systematic comparison methodology |
| *Mežduslavjanski jezik* | Ladislav Podmele | 1950s | 20th-century naturalistic attempt |

These projects are incorporated into the historical record of the language; they are not normatively superseded by this specification, as they were never normative documents. Full historical context is provided in the relevant section
