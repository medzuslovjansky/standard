# Sample Texts and Communication Scenarios

This appendix presents annotated sample texts drawn from authentic Interslavic sources. Each entry provides the text in standard Latin orthography and an English translation. Brief grammar notes highlight features that recur across the Interslavic system.

Sources: dialogues adapted from the Ranmaru educational series (files 56–74); prose relay texts compiled by Jan van Steenbergen.

---

## Moj najlěpši prijatelj
**Topic:** Describing a friend; personal appearance and character

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Moj najlěpši prijatelj**] |
| A | :isv[Imaš li najlěpšego prijatelja?] |
| B | :isv[Råzuměje sę!] |
| A | :isv[Kako je jego imę?] |
| B | :isv[Imę mu je Miša.] |
| A | :isv[Kako on izględa?] |
| B | :isv[On je vysoky. Ima světle vlåsy i modre oči. Mysljų, že on je veljmi lěpy.] |
| A | :isv[Začto on ti sę podoba?] |
| B | :isv[On je istinny optimist. Ako li imam problem, on vsegda vě, čto kazati. On je veljmi spokojny. Takože ima odličny smysl za humor.] |
| A | :isv[Koliko dòlgo vy sę poznate?] |
| B | :isv[Poznamo sę uže mnogo lět. Studovali jesmo zajedno.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **My best friend** |
| A | Do you have a best friend? |
| B | Of course! |
| A | What is his name? |
| B | His name is Misha. |
| A | What does he look like? |
| B | He is tall. He has fair hair and blue eyes. I think he is very good-looking. |
| A | Why do you like him? |
| B | He is a true optimist. If I have a problem, he always knows what to say. He is very calm. He also has an excellent sense of humor. |
| A | How long have you known each other? |
| B | We have known each other for many years already. We studied together. |

**Grammar notes.** The superlative :isv[najlěpši] (best) uses the prefix :isv[naj-] added to the comparative :isv[lěpši]. The reflexive particle :isv[sę] in :isv[poznate sę] and :isv[Råzuměje sę] signals a mutual or impersonal action. :isv[Imę mu je Miša] uses the dative of interest (:isv[mu]) to express possession: "his name is Misha" (literally, "the name to-him is Misha"). :isv[Mysljų] is first-person singular present of :isv[mysljati] (to think).

## Obyčny denj
**Topic:** Daily routine; present tense narration

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Obyčny denj**] |
| A | :isv[Čto ty dělaš každy denj utrom?] |
| B | :isv[Ja ljubjų vstavati rano. Ja vsegda vstajų v 7 časov i běgam v parku. Podčas běga, obyčno ja slušam muzykų na mp3. Potom sę myjų pod dušem i zautrakajų.] |
| A | :isv[Čto ty obyčno imaš za zautrak?] |
| B | :isv[Obyčno ja pijų čaj i jěm sendvič. Poněkda, kògda imam malo vrěmena, ja samo pijų kavų s mlěkom.] |
| A | :isv[Jězdiš li na råbotų avtom?] |
| B | :isv[Ne, prědvyšam pųtovati metrom.] |
| A | :isv[Kdě råbotaš i čto dělaš?] |
| B | :isv[Ja råbotam v kompaniji. Každy denj råzgovarjam s klientami i odpovědam na e-maily. Moja råbota ne je veljmi zanimliva.] |
| A | :isv[Čto dělaš poslě råboty?] |
| B | :isv[Poslě råboty idų do doma. Doma kuham večerjų, a potom ględam noviny ili čitam knigų.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **An ordinary day** |
| A | What do you do every morning? |
| B | I love to get up early. I always get up at 7 o'clock and jog in the park. While jogging I usually listen to music on my mp3 player. Then I shower and have breakfast. |
| A | What do you usually have for breakfast? |
| B | I usually drink tea and eat a sandwich. Sometimes, when I have little time, I only drink coffee with milk. |
| A | Do you go to work by car? |
| B | No, I prefer to travel by metro. |
| A | Where do you work and what do you do? |
| B | I work in a company. Every day I talk with clients and answer e-mails. My work is not very interesting. |
| A | What do you do after work? |
| B | After work I go home. At home I cook dinner, and then I watch the news or read a book. |

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Poněkda] (sometimes) and :isv[podčas] (during, while) are common adverbial time expressions. The verb :isv[prědvyšati] (to prefer) takes an infinitive complement. :isv[Doma] (at home) functions as a locative adverb without a preposition. Accusatives with motion prepositions: :isv[na råbotų], :isv[do doma].

## Svobodno vrěmę
**Topic:** Leisure activities; weekends

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Svobodno vrěmę**] |
| A | :isv[Čto ty dělaš v svobodno vrěmę?] |
| B | :isv[V svobodno vrěmę ja ljubjų hoditi v basen. Mi sę podoba plavati. Ovo je dobro za zdråvje.] |
| A | :isv[Hodiš li sama ili s prijateljami?] |
| B | :isv[Moji prijatelji ne ljubęt plavati. Zato obyčno hođų sama.] |
| A | :isv[A čto ty ljubiš dělati vikendom?] |
| B | :isv[V vikend ljubjų dòlgo spati, ibo v tečeńju tydnja vsegda vstajų veljmi rano. Takože mi sę podoba hoditi v fitness klub. V sobotų strěćam sę s prijateljami i idemo na diskotekų.] |
| A | :isv[A čto ty ljubiš dělati v nedělji?] |
| B | :isv[V nedělji ja ljubjų odpočivati. Mi sę podoba ględati televizijų ili čitati knigų. Veljmi ljubjų takože teatr i kino. Nedělja je idealny denj, da sę odpravim v kino.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **Free time** |
| A | What do you do in your free time? |
| B | In my free time I love going to the swimming pool. I enjoy swimming. It is good for one's health. |
| A | Do you go alone or with friends? |
| B | My friends don't like to swim. So I usually go alone. |
| A | And what do you like to do at weekends? |
| B | At the weekend I like to sleep in late, because during the week I always get up very early. I also enjoy going to the fitness club. On Saturday I meet up with friends and we go to a disco. |
| A | And what do you like to do on Sunday? |
| B | On Sunday I like to relax. I enjoy watching TV or reading a book. I also really love the theatre and cinema. Sunday is the ideal day to go to the cinema. |

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Mi sę podoba] (I enjoy / it pleases me) uses the dative subject :isv[mi] — the verb agrees with the infinitive or noun, not with the experiencer. :isv[Zato] (therefore, that is why) is a causal connective. :isv[Da sę odpravim] introduces a purpose clause using :isv[da] + present subjunctive.

## Odkųdy jesi?
**Topic:** Meeting someone new; origins and language learning

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Odkųdy jesi?**] |
| A | :isv[Ahoj! Jesi li nova?] |
| B | :isv[Ahoj! Da, dneś je moj pŕvy denj v školě. Kako sę zòveš?] |
| A | :isv[Zòvų sę Stefani. A kako ty sę zòveš?] |
| B | :isv[Zòvų sę Marta. Prijatno mi je tę poznati. Odkųdy jesi?] |
| A | :isv[Jesm iz SŠA. A ty?] |
| B | :isv[Jesm iz Išpanije. Žijų ovdě uže 2 měsęca.] |
| A | :isv[Veljmi dobro govoriš russky.] |
| B | :isv[Dękujų. Moja najlěpša prijateljka je rusinka i ona mę uči.] |
| A | :isv[Udačnica. Može byti, tòž trěbam russkogo prijatelja, da byh lučše govorila.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **Where are you from?** |
| A | Hi! Are you new here? |
| B | Hi! Yes, today is my first day at school. What is your name? |
| A | My name is Stefani. And what is your name? |
| B | My name is Marta. Nice to meet you. Where are you from? |
| A | I am from the USA. And you? |
| B | I am from Spain. I have been living here for 2 months already. |
| A | You speak Russian very well. |
| B | Thank you. My best friend is Russian and she teaches me. |
| A | You are lucky. Maybe I also need a Russian friend to speak better. |

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Zòvų sę] (my name is; I am called) is the reflexive construction using first-person singular of :isv[zòvati] (to call). :isv[Trěbati] (to need) here governs a genitive object (:isv[russkogo prijatelja]). The conditional :isv[da byh govorila] uses the auxiliary :isv[byh] (conditional first-person singular) plus the l-participle.

## Pivo, prošų
**Topic:** Ordering in a bar; asking for help

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Pivo, prošų** *(V baru)*] |
| A | :isv[Dajte mi dva piva, prošų.] |
| B | :isv[Kakòvo pivo? Temno ili světlo?] |
| A | :isv[Čto? Žalj mi je, ja ne govorjų russky. Samo pivo.] |
| B | :isv[Pa, sdaje sę mi, že vy govorite russky.] |
| A | :isv[Govorjų trohų i ne råzumějų, čto vy pytate.] |
| B | :isv[Kakòvo pivo? Ukažite pŕstom.] |
| A | :isv[Ovo za 50 rubljev.] |
| B | :isv[Dobro. Dva piva. Bųde 100 rubljev.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **A beer, please** *(In a bar)* |
| A | Give me two beers, please. |
| B | What kind of beer? Dark or light? |
| A | What? I'm sorry, I don't speak Russian. Just beer. |
| B | Well, it seems to me that you do speak Russian. |
| A | I speak a little and I don't understand what you are asking. |
| B | What kind of beer? Point with your finger. |
| A | This one for 50 roubles. |
| B | Fine. Two beers. That will be 100 roubles. |

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Sdaje sę mi] (it seems to me) is an impersonal expression with dative :isv[mi]. :isv[Pytati] (to ask, to question) is distinct from :isv[prositi] (to request). :isv[Trohų] (a little) is used adverbially; the nasal vowel reflects the Proto-Slavic *ǫ* preserved in Interslavic orthography. The imperative :isv[dajte] and :isv[ukažite] use the standard second-person plural polite form.

## Ostrižeńje
**Topic:** At the hairdresser; appearance and colour

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Ostrižeńje**] |
| A | :isv[Zdråvo! Hòtěla byh sę ostrići.] |
| B | :isv[Sadite sę, prošų. Kakòvų pričeskų vy želate?] |
| A | :isv[Ja želala byh pričeskų kako děvčina na tom foto. Ględajte, ima grivkų i kråtke vlåsy. Čto myslite, bųde li ta pričeska izględati na mně dobro?] |
| B | :isv[Da, mysljų tako. Imate ovalno lice i grivka bųde izględati u vas dobro. Ale jeste blondinka, a děvčina na foto je brunetka. Želate li farbovati vlåsy?] |
| A | :isv[Želala byh farbovati v rudy. Čto myslite?] |
| B | :isv[Mysljų, že rudy vam ne bųde dobro. Može byti, temno rudy. Kako o ovom časopisu. Podoba li sę vam tòj kolor?] |
| A | :isv[Veljmi originalny kolor! Sųglåšų sę. Večeraś mę moj mųž ne pozna.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **A haircut** |
| A | Hello! I would like to have my hair cut. |
| B | Please sit down. What kind of hairstyle would you like? |
| A | I would like the hairstyle like the girl in that photo. Look, she has a fringe and short hair. What do you think — will that hairstyle look good on me? |
| B | Yes, I think so. You have an oval face and the fringe will look good on you. But you are a blonde, and the girl in the photo is a brunette. Would you like to colour your hair? |
| A | I would like to dye it red. What do you think? |
| B | I think red won't suit you. Perhaps dark red. Like in this magazine. Do you like that colour? |
| A | A very original colour! I agree. This evening my husband won't recognise me. |

**Grammar notes.** The conditional :isv[hòtěla byh] (I would like) is feminine form, with the l-participle :isv[hòtěla] agreeing with the female speaker. :isv[Večeraś] (this evening, tonight) uses the suffix *-aś* indicating "today" (cf. :isv[dneś] — today). :isv[Sųglåšati sę] (to agree) is a reflexive verb.

## Moj rodinny album
**Topic:** Family; physical resemblance; future plans

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Moj rodinny album**] |
| A | :isv[Želaš viděti moj rodinny album?] |
| B | :isv[S radosťjų.] |
| A | :isv[Ovo sųt moji roditelji. Tu sųt veljmi mlådi. Ovo je jih svaťba.] |
| B | :isv[Izględaš velje kako tvoja mati. Imate jednakòve oči i usměh.] |
| A | :isv[Mysliš tako? Vsi srodniki mojego otca kažųt, že izględam velje kako on. Imamo jednakòvy nos. Ględaj, ovo je moja babica. Ovdě ima 25 lět.] |
| B | :isv[Kakòva krasavica! Tęžko je věriti, že ty i ja bųdemo imati někògda tòž 70 lět.] |
| A | :isv[Raděje ne råzmysljam o tom.] |
| B | :isv[A kto je tòj privlěkateljny hlåpec?] |
| A | :isv[Ovo je moj brat. On je morjak. Uniforma izględa dobro na njem, ale ne?] |
| B | :isv[Zělo. I kdě je sejčas?] |
| A | :isv[On plava po Srědizemskomu morju. Už skoro prijěde v odpusk. Imati bųdemo zabavų za pråzdnovańje jego prijězda. Prijdeš?] |
| B | :isv[Bezsųmno!] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **My family album** |
| A | Would you like to see my family album? |
| B | With pleasure. |
| A | These are my parents. They are very young here. This is their wedding. |
| B | You look a lot like your mother. You have the same eyes and smile. |
| A | You think so? All my father's relatives say that I look a lot like him. We have the same nose. Look, this is my grandmother. She is 25 years old here. |
| B | What a beauty! It is hard to believe that you and I will also be 70 years old someday. |
| A | I would rather not think about that. |
| B | And who is that attractive young man? |
| A | This is my brother. He is a sailor. The uniform looks good on him, doesn't it? |
| B | Very much so. And where is he now? |
| A | He is sailing on the Mediterranean Sea. He will be coming home on leave soon. We will have a party to celebrate his arrival. Will you come? |
| B | Certainly! |

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Velje] (a lot, much) is an adverb used in comparisons. :isv[Raděje] (rather, preferably) is a comparative adverb from :isv[råd] (glad, willing). :isv[Bezsųmno] (certainly, without doubt) uses the prefix :isv[bez-] (without) plus :isv[sųmněńje] (doubt). The future compound :isv[imati bųdemo] places the infinitive before the auxiliary.

## Kakòvo strånno hobbi!
**Topic:** Unusual hobbies; comparison and surprise

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Kakòvo strånno hobbi!**] |
| A | :isv[Imaš li hobbi?] |
| B | :isv[Imam. Ljubim pleteńje i sambo.] |
| A | :isv[Sambo? Ne je li slučajno něko bojevo uměńje.] |
| B | :isv[Da, da, to je russko bojevo uměńje.] |
| A | :isv[Ale pleteńje i sambo sųt popòlno ine věći! Pleteńje je råzumlivo. Mnogo děvčęt ljubęt pleteńje. No sambo! Udiviteljno! I koliko dòlgo praktikuješ sambo?] |
| B | :isv[Uže desęť lět. Počęla jesm praktikovati sambo, kògda jesm studovala v školě. Zato sųt sę hlåpci iz našego klassa vsegda bojali mę.] |
| A | :isv[Dobro. A tvoje drugo hobbi?] |
| B | :isv[Pleteńje je veljmi uspokojęća dějateljnosť. Moja babica mę je naučila. Kromě togo, to je sposob ščęditi pěnęzi. Ja pletų dlja sebe modne šali, šapky i svetry. Ględaj, jesm splela sama tòj sveter. Podoba li ti sę to?] |
| A | :isv[Zělo je... originalny.] |
| B | :isv[Dobro, i jestli ti sę to ne podoba, možeš povědati pravdų, ja tę ne bųdų biti!] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **What a strange hobby!** |
| A | Do you have a hobby? |
| B | I do. I love knitting and sambo. |
| A | Sambo? Isn't that some kind of martial art? |
| B | Yes, yes, it is a Russian martial art. |
| A | But knitting and sambo are completely different things! Knitting is understandable. Many girls love knitting. But sambo! Amazing! And how long have you been practising sambo? |
| B | Ten years already. I started practising sambo when I was studying at school. That is why the boys in our class were always afraid of me. |
| A | I see. And your other hobby? |
| B | Knitting is a very calming activity. My grandmother taught me. Besides, it is a way to save money. I knit fashionable scarves, hats and sweaters for myself. Look, I knitted this sweater myself. Do you like it? |
| A | It is very... original. |
| B | Fine, and if you don't like it, you can tell the truth — I won't hit you! |

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Bojevo uměńje] (martial art; literally "combat skill") is a common compound. :isv[Děvčę] (girl) belongs to the neuter *-ę* stem declension; its genitive plural :isv[děvčęt] shows the characteristic zero ending with vowel lengthening. :isv[Ščęditi pěnęzi] (to save money) uses the accusative plural :isv[pěnęzi] from the i-stem plural :isv[pěnęzi] (money). :isv[Uspokojęća] is a present active participle (calming).

## Velika prośba
**Topic:** Asking a favour; describing people's appearance

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Velika prośba**] |
| A | :isv[Leno, imam k tobě velikų prośbų! Možeš li večeraś strětnųti mojų prijateljkų v aeroportu? Nemam vrěmena.] |
| B | :isv[Råzuměje sę. Ne je problem. Kako ona izględa?] |
| A | :isv[Ona je privlěkateljna blondinka s zelenymi očami. Ona ne je vysoka. Ljubi nositi sportivnų odeđų. Bųde imati jarko oranževy kufer.] |
| B | :isv[Imam nadejų, že ne bųde nekoliko blondinok s oranževymi kuframi.] |
| A | :isv[Ne trevoži sę, ja opišų jej tvoj izględ.] |
| B | :isv[Povědaj jej, že ja bųdų v koričnevoj kurtkě i černyh džinsah.] |
| A | :isv[Jej bųde dostatòčno znati, že jesi skoro dva metra vysoka.] |
| B | :isv[Imaš pravdų, tęžko je mę ne primětiti.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **A big favour** |
| A | Lena, I have a big favour to ask you! Can you meet my friend at the airport this evening? I don't have the time. |
| B | Of course. No problem. What does she look like? |
| A | She is an attractive blonde with green eyes. She is not tall. She likes wearing sportswear. She will have a bright orange suitcase. |
| B | I hope there won't be several blondes with orange suitcases. |
| A | Don't worry, I'll describe your appearance to her. |
| B | Tell her that I'll be wearing a brown jacket and black jeans. |
| A | It will be enough for her to know that you are nearly two metres tall. |
| B | You are right, it is hard not to notice me. |

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Imam k tobě prośbų] (I have a request of you) uses the prepositional phrase :isv[k] + dative for the person addressed in a request. :isv[Skoro] (almost, nearly) in this context is an approximative adverb. :isv[Tęžko je] (it is difficult) is an impersonal construction with infinitive complement. Instrumental plural in clothing: :isv[v koričnevoj kurtkě i černyh džinsah] uses locative for "in/wearing".

## Kamo pojědem v odpusk?
**Topic:** Planning a holiday; disagreement and compromise

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Kamo pojědem v odpusk?**] |
| A | :isv[Ja ne hòćų jěhati v Egipt. Jězdili jesmo minųlym godom i mi ne je spodobalo sę.] |
| B | :isv[A mi je spodobalo sę. Hòćų snova onamo pojěhati.] |
| A | :isv[Teplo morje, odlično nyrjańje.] |
| B | :isv[Ale tam je prěmnogo goręće v sej sezon. Može, pojědemo v bolje hlådno město.] |
| A | :isv[Na Sěverny polus?] |
| B | :isv[Ne rěči bezsmyslice! Pojědimo v Evropų, na priměr, v Rim. Možemo posětiti toliko kulturnyh měst. Poznamo toliko zanimlivogo.] |
| A | :isv[Ja ne govorjų italijansky.] |
| B | :isv[Arabsky ty takože ne govoriš, a hòćeš pojěhati v Egipt!] |
| A | :isv[To ne je tòjže stvår...] |
| B | :isv[Jědų v Rim i točka.] |
| A | :isv[Nu, dobro... Ja takože.] |

### English translation

| Line | English |
|------|---------|
| | **Where shall we go on holiday?** |
| A | I don't want to go to Egypt. We went last year and I didn't enjoy it. |
| B | I enjoyed it. I want to go there again. |
| A | Warm sea, excellent diving. |
| B | But it is far too hot there at this time of year. Perhaps we should go somewhere cooler. |
| A | To the North Pole? |
| B | Don't talk nonsense! Let's go to Europe, for example to Rome. We could visit so many cultural sites. We would learn so much interesting stuff. |
| A | I don't speak Italian. |
| B | You don't speak Arabic either, yet you want to go to Egypt! |
| A | That is not the same thing... |
| B | I am going to Rome and that's that. |
| A | Well, fine... Me too. |

**Grammar notes.** The directional :isv[kamo] (where to, whither) contrasts with :isv[kdě] (where, at what place). :isv[Prěmnogo] (far too much, excessively) uses the prefix :isv[prě-] (over-, too). :isv[Tòjže stvår] (the same thing) employs the identity particle *-že* attached to the demonstrative. :isv[I točka] (and that is that; and full stop) is a colloquial emphatic ending.

## Nenaviđų domovų råbotų!
**Topic:** Household chores; narrating past events; in-laws

### Interslavic

| Line | Interslavic |
|------|-------------|
| | :isv[**Nenaviđų domovų råbotų!**] |

## Russky medvěď
**Topic:** Facts about Russia; myth vs. reality; prose monologue with joke

Some foreigners think that bears walk along the streets in Russia. Of course, that is not true! Bears live in forests and do not like people.

An encounter with a bear can be very dangerous. Russian people love going into the forest to pick mushrooms and berries. They do it carefully, because bears also love berries and mushrooms and may attack a person. A bear eats everything: berries, fish, meat, and even insects. He especially loves honey.

In winter bears usually sleep, because the weather is too cold and there is no food. While the bear sleeps, it can lose 80 kilograms in weight! Russian people look very much like bears. In winter they also become slow and sleep a lot, because outside it is far too cold and dark.

That is why Russians love bears and call them "mishki". Misha was the mascot of the Summer Olympics of 1980 in Moscow.

**Bear joke:** Cold winter. A bear wanders through the forest, stops from time to time, bangs his head against a tree and cries: "Why, oh why did I drink so much coffee?!"

### Interslavic

:isv[Někòtori ćuđezemci mnęt, že v Rusiji medvědi hodęt po ulicah. Råzuměje sę, to ne je pravda! Medvědi žijųt v lěsah i ne ljubęt ljudij.]

:isv[Strěća s medvědem može byti duže opasna. Russki ljudi ljubęt hoditi v lěs i sòbirati griby i jagody. Oni dělajųt to s vnimańjem, ibo medvědi takože ljubęt jagody i griby i mogųt napasti na člověka. Medvěď jěst vse: jagody, rybų, męso i daže insektov. Osoblivo on ljubi med.]

:isv[V zimų medvědi obyčno spęt, ibo vrěmę je prěmnogo hlådno i nema jědy. Dokolě medvěď spi, on može shudnųti na 80 kilogrammov! Russki ljudi duže izględajųt kako medvědi. V zimų oni takože stajųt medli i mnogo spęt, ibo vo vně je prěmnogo hlådno i temno.]

:isv[Zato že rusi ljubęt medvědev i zòvųt jih «miški». Miša je talisman lětnjej olimpiady 1980 goda v Moskvě.]

:isv[**Směška o medvědi:** Hlådna zima. Medvěď blųka po lěsu, poněkda sę zaustavja, udarja glåvojų po drěvesu i kriči: «Nu, začto, začto jesm pil tako mnogo kavy?!»]

**Grammar notes.** This text demonstrates continuous prose style. :isv[Dokolě] (while, as long as) is a temporal conjunction. :isv[Shudnųti] (to lose weight) uses the prefix :isv[s-] for completion. :isv[Zòvųt jih «miški»] (they call them "mishki") uses the accusative :isv[jih] (them) as the direct object with the instrumental or nominative predicate in a naming construction. The joke uses the perfect :isv[jesm pil] (I have drunk / I drank) with an exclamatory question.

## Najhlådnějši gråd na Zemji
**Topic:** Extreme geography; community life; descriptive prose

### Interslavic

:isv[Najhlådnějše město v světu je russko selo Ojmjakon. Ovo selo sę nahodi na Iztoku. Tam žijųt okolo 500 ljudij. V zimų obyčna temperatura je minus 50 gradusov. Raz v ovom selu temperatura je byla minus 71 gradus! No žitelji sela sųt privykli k hlådų. Sdě vsi jedin drugogo znajųt. Vsi jedin drugomu pomogajųt.]

:isv[Obljųbjeno město strěći je magazin, kdě prodajųt svěži hlěb. Selo nema bara, ibo poslě 18:00 časov ne je dopušćeno prodavati ni vodkų, ni pivo. Pijany člověk može umreti na ulici od hlåda veljmi brzo…]

### English translation

The coldest place in the world is the Russian village of Oymyakon. This village is located in the East. About 500 people live there. In winter the normal temperature is minus 50 degrees. Once in this village the temperature was minus 71 degrees! But the villagers are used to the cold. Here everyone knows everyone. Everyone helps one another.

The favourite meeting place is the shop where they sell fresh bread. The village has no bar, because after 18:00 it is not permitted to sell either vodka or beer. A drunk person could die on the street from the cold very quickly…

In winter the day lasts only 3 hours. And in summer it is light even at night. In March there is a festival in the village. Many foreigners come to this festival. They want to experience what minus 50 degrees feels like.

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Jedin drugogo / jedin drugomu] (each other, one another) is the reciprocal construction using the indefinite numeral :isv[jedin] (one) with matching case forms. :isv[Ne je dopušćeno] (it is not permitted) is an impersonal passive construction. :isv[Ni... ni...] (neither... nor...) is the negative correlative.

## Sankt-Peterburg
**Topic:** History and culture of a city; descriptive prose

### Interslavic

:isv[Sankt-Peterburg je jedin iz najkrasivših grådov Rusije. On byl je osnovany imperatorom Petrom I (pŕvym). Imperator je rěšil izbudovati gråd sdě, da by odkryti dlja Rusije «okno v Evropų».]

:isv[Prěđe na ovom městě bylo je blåto, zato budovati gråd bylo je veljmi tęžko. Mnogo ljudij je umŕlo, kògda budovali tòj gråd. Zato i rěkųt, že to je «gråd na kosťah».]

:isv[V Sankt-Peterburgě je mnogo krasivyh palacev, muzejev i cŕkvij. Sdě sę nahodi znamenita Jantarova komnata. V lěto v Sankt-Peterburgě byvajųt «běle noći».]

:isv[To je dnji, kògda noćjų vně je světlo skoro råvno, kako dnjem. To je najbolje popularno vrěmę dlja turistov. Noćjų na ulicah je mnogo ljudij i sdaje sę, že veś gråd ne spi.]

### English translation

Saint Petersburg is one of the most beautiful cities in Russia. It was founded by Emperor Peter I (the First). The Emperor decided to build a city here in order to open a "window onto Europe" for Russia.

Previously this place was a swamp, so building the city was very difficult. Many people died when they were building that city. That is why they say it is "a city built on bones".

In Saint Petersburg there are many beautiful palaces, museums and churches. The famous Amber Room is located here. In summer Saint Petersburg has "white nights".

These are days when at night outside it is almost as light as during the day. This is the most popular time for tourists. At night there are many people on the streets and it seems as if the whole city is not sleeping.

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Da by odkryti] uses the purpose construction :isv[da] + conditional. :isv[Blåto] (swamp, marsh) is an archaic/poetic word, preserved in Interslavic. :isv[Cŕkvij] is the genitive plural of :isv[cŕkva] (church), showing the consonant-cluster form *cŕk-* with vocalic *r*. :isv[Sdaje sę] (it seems) is an impersonal reflexive.

## Prividěńje
**Topic:** Ghost story; historical legend; Saint Petersburg

### Interslavic

:isv[Jedno iz najznamenitějših prividěnij Sankt-Peterburga je prividěńje Pavla Pŕvogo. Ovo prividěńje žije v Mihajlovskom zamku, kdě žil i byl je ubijen imperator.]

:isv[Raz podčas bala imperator naglo omdlěl. Kògda on je sę sbudil, to ględnųl s bezumnymi očami na vsih gostev i kazal: «Napravdų udušęt mę?» Kako rezultat, vse je sę i slučilo točno tako.]

:isv[Kògda imperator imal je 47 lět, on byl je udušeny v svojem zamku. Několiko dnjev prěd smŕťjų, on je rěkl, že vidi kròv na bělyh stěnah svojej spaľnji. Poslě smŕti imperatora v zamku dòlgo vrěmę nikto ne je žil. Gråd je byl pòlny strašnyh povědòk o prividěńji.]

:isv[Nyně v Mihajlovskom zamku sę nahodi Russky muzej. Råbotniky muzeja ne ljubęt ostavati tam na večer. Oni govoręt, že v pustom dvorcu sę odkryvajųt i zakryvajųt dveri i skripi podloga. Stråži poněkda vidęt figurų člověka so svěćejų v rukě. Kògda tako byvaje oni tiho rěkųt jej: «Dobra noć, Vaše Veličenstvo».]

### English translation

One of the most famous ghosts of Saint Petersburg is the ghost of Paul the First. This ghost lives in the Mikhailovsky Castle, where the Emperor lived and was murdered.

Once during a ball, the Emperor suddenly fainted. When he came round, he looked at all the guests with wild eyes and said: "Will they really strangle me?" As a result, that is exactly what happened.

When the Emperor was 47 years old, he was strangled in his own castle. Several days before his death he said that he could see blood on the white walls of his bedroom. After the Emperor's death, no one lived in the castle for a long time. The city was full of terrifying stories about the ghost.

Now the Russian Museum is located in the Mikhailovsky Castle. The museum staff do not like staying there in the evening. They say that in the empty palace doors open and close by themselves and the floor creaks. The guards sometimes see the figure of a man holding a candle. When this happens, they say quietly to him: "Good night, Your Majesty."

**Grammar notes.** :isv[Omdlěti] (to faint) and :isv[sbuditi sę] (to come round, to wake up) are prefixed verbs of the body. :isv[Naglo] (suddenly, unexpectedly) is the adverb from :isv[nagly]. :isv[Udušati] (to strangle, to smother) uses the prefix :isv[u-] for completion. :isv[So svěćejų v rukě] (with a candle in hand) uses the instrumental :isv[svěćejų] and the locative :isv[v rukě]. :isv[Vaše Veličenstvo] (Your Majesty) is a fixed honorific form.

## Naše selo — The Pan-Slavic Relay
**Topic:** Village life; nostalgia; literary prose

This text originates from the Pan-Slavic Relay of September–October 2009, an event in which participants in various Slavic constructed languages translated a passage in relay fashion. The source passage was taken from a nineteenth-century English essay by Mary Russell Mitford. The Interslavic version below is by Jan van Steenbergen, representing the Slovianski stage of the relay; it is included here as a literary register sample distinct from the conversational dialogues above.

### Interslavic — Slovianski

:isv[Iz vsih možlivih ideal'nih mest, v ktorih živut ljudi, ja najviše-mnogo ljubim male selo, daleko od šumnogo groda, s jego maloju společnostju. Ono ne ima prepolnenih blokov, tol'ko male drevjane budinki – proste i malovatel'ne mesto dlja žitja, s žiteljami, ktorih obliča sut rovno znajome kak cveti v našem sade. To je zatvoreni svet s nemnogo ljudami, blizko sojedinenimi kak mrovki v mrovkovoj kope, pčeli v pčel'nike, ovci v ovečkom gumne, muhi v mušskom gnezde ili morniki na korable, gde vsekto znaje vsekogo i vsekto je znani od vsekogo, gde každi interesuje sja v každom i každi može imat' nadeju, čo nekto interesuje sja v njego.]

:isv[Kak milo bilo bi zanurit' sja v to serdečne čuvstvo ljubenija i neznanih običajov, sobrat' sja i bit' prijateli s timi vsimi jedinstvenimi ljudjami okolo nas! Tak, čo mi budeme znat' i ljubit' vse kuti i obroti tenjovih ulic i solnečne pokrivne kanali, prez ktore mi prehodime celi den'.]

:isv[Libokak, mala social'na grupa, ktora je sel'ska společnost', je to, čemu poezija i proza djakujut najviše-mnogo. Dolga, roztjagnuta droga, bludžuča sja v milem, teplom dne i prehodžuča veliku, široku drogu polnu avt i tirov. Vi ne hočete putovat' s mnoju, droge čitateli? Put' ne bude dolga. Mi počeme na konce dolini i odtud poideme v goru.]

### English translation (after Steeven Radzikowski)

Of all possible ideal places for people to live, the one I love the most is a small village, far from the noise of the city, with its small community. It does not have crowded apartment blocks, only small wooden cottages — a simple and picturesque place to live, with inhabitants whose faces are as familiar as the flowers in our garden. It is an enclosed world with few people, closely united like ants in an ant-hill, bees in a hive, sheep in a sheep-fold, flies in a swarm, or sailors on a ship — where everyone knows everyone and everyone is known by everyone, where each person takes an interest in every other and each person may hope that someone takes an interest in him.

How pleasant it would be to immerse oneself in that warm feeling of love and unfamiliar customs, to gather together and be friends with all those unique people around us! So that we would come to know and love all the corners and turns of the shaded streets and sun-drenched meadows that we pass through every day.

In any case, a small social group — which is a village community — is what poetry and prose value most of all. A long, stretched-out road, winding its way through a mild, warm day and crossing a large wide road full of cars and lorries. Do you not want to travel with me, dear reader? The journey will not be long. We start at the end of the valley and from there we go uphill.

**Grammar notes.** This relay text represents an early stage of what became modern Interslavic (then called Slovianski). It uses *-t'* for the soft sign in infinitives and *sja* as the reflexive particle, reflecting the Slavic-normalised orthography of the period. Several lexical choices (*libokak* for "in any case", *djakujut* for "are grateful for", *drogu polnu avt* for "road full of cars") illustrate how literary and colloquial registers handled internationalisms. The passage also demonstrates complex relative clause nesting and the deliberate pastoral register that distinguishes literary Interslavic from the conversational dialogues in sections 1–19.
