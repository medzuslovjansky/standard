# False Friends Register
:::info

A false friend (_lažny prijatelj_) is a word that looks or sounds similar to a word in another Slavic language but differs substantially in meaning. Because Interslavic draws its vocabulary from the common Slavic stock, a learner who already knows one or more Slavic languages may occasionally misread an Interslavic word through the lens of their own language. This register documents the most consequential such pairs.

Entries are sorted alphabetically by Interslavic form. The columns record: the Interslavic word and its meaning in Interslavic; the confusable form in a specific Slavic language; the language in question; and the meaning that form carries in that language. A usage note explains the scope of the conflict and advises caution where it matters most.

:::

## How to use this register

The register does not claim that an Interslavic word is wrong or ambiguous in isolation. Interslavic meaning is determined by the specification; the foreign-language meanings listed here are purely those that a native speaker of the language in question might inadvertently project onto an Interslavic text. The practical risk of a misunderstanding depends on context: some pairs produce only a slight semantic shift, while others could reverse the entire sense of a sentence.

Abbreviations used in the Language column: **BG** = Bulgarian; **BS/HR/SR** = Bosnian/Croatian/Serbian; **CS** = Czech; **MK** = Macedonian; **PL** = Polish; **RU** = Russian; **SK** = Slovak; **SL** = Slovene; **UK** = Ukrainian.

## Notes on methodology

The entries in this register are drawn from three main sources: (1) documented false-friend pairs discussed in Jan van Steenbergen's Interslavic design criteria documentation (particularly the _False friends_ subsection); (2) the English Wikibooks list _False Friends of the Slavist_; and (3) the general comparative Slavic linguistics literature. Priority was given to pairs where the meaning divergence is sufficient to cause genuine miscommunication in context, rather than minor semantic shading.

Not all potential false friends are listed. Pairs where the meanings are closely related and only differ in degree or register are generally omitted. Pairs where the Interslavic word is so rare in practice that the risk of confusion is negligible are also excluded.

This register should be updated as the Interslavic lexical corpus grows and as community experience identifies new problem pairs.
