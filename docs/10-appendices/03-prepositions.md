## Motion vs. Static Alternations

Several prepositions govern different cases depending on motion (direction) vs. static location:

| Preposition | Static (location) | Motion (direction) |
|-------------|------------------|--------------------|
| :isv[na] | locative: :isv[na stolu] "on the table" | accusative: :isv[na stol] "onto the table" |
| :isv[v/vo] | locative: :isv[v domu] "in the house" | accusative: :isv[v dom] "into the house" |
| :isv[pod] | instrumental: :isv[pod stolom] "under the table" | accusative: :isv[pod stol] "under the table (motion)" |
| :isv[nad] | instrumental: :isv[nad stolom] "above the table" | accusative: :isv[nad stol] "up above the table (motion)" |
| :isv[prěd] | instrumental: :isv[prěd domom] "in front of the house" | genitive/accusative: :isv[prěd dom] "(motion) in front of" |
| :isv[za] | instrumental: :isv[za domom] "behind the house" | accusative: :isv[za dom] "behind the house (motion)" |
| :isv[meže/medžu] | instrumental: :isv[meže dvoma domami] "between two houses" | — |

## Selected Preposition Notes

- :isv[bez] "without" always governs genitive: :isv[bez vody] "without water"
- :isv[do] "to, until" always governs genitive: :isv[do Moskvy] "to Moscow", :isv[do večera] "until evening"
- :isv[iz] "out of" always governs genitive: :isv[iz doma] "out of the house"
- :isv[k/ko] "to, toward" always governs dative: :isv[k mně] "to me", :isv[ko dveri] "to the door"
- :isv[od] "from" always governs genitive: :isv[od brata] "from (a) brother"
- :isv[pri] "at, near, during" always governs locative: :isv[pri rěcě] "at the river"
- :isv[s/so] + instrumental = "together with": :isv[s bratom] "with (a) brother"
- :isv[u] "at, by" always governs genitive: :isv[u mene] "at my place / I have"
