# 5.4. Adverbs

This section specifies the formation, comparison, and use of adverbs in Interslavic. All normative rules use the terms MUST, SHALL, SHOULD, and MAY as defined in RFC 2119.

---

## 5.4.1. Formation from Adjectives

Adverbs in Interslavic are formed from adjectives by replacing the adjectival ending with the suffix **:isv[-o]** (after hard stems) or **:isv[-e]** (after soft stems — i.e., stems ending in :isv[š], :isv[ž], :isv[č], or :isv[j]). The resulting form is identical to the neuter singular nominative/accusative of the corresponding adjective.

| Adjective | Adverb | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| :isv[dobry] | :isv[dobro] | "well" |
| :isv[prosty] | :isv[prosto] | "simply" |
| :isv[tihy] | :isv[tiho] | "quietly" |
| :isv[svěži] | :isv[svěže] | "freshly" |
| :isv[gorjači] | :isv[gorjače] | "hotly" |

The short form of adjectives (§5.3.3.1) MUST NOT be confused with the corresponding adverb. The adverb is always identical to the neuter singular form of the long adjective.

---

## 5.4.2. Comparative and Superlative of Adverbs

### 5.4.2.1. Synthetic Comparative Adverb (:isv[-ěje] / :isv[-eje])

Comparative adverbs are derived from the synthetic adjective comparative (§5.3.6.2) by replacing the comparative suffix **:isv[-ši]** with **:isv[-e]**, yielding the ending **:isv[-ěje]** (after hard stems) or **:isv[-eje]** (after soft stems):

| Positive adverb | Comparative adverb | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| :isv[novo] | :isv[nověje] | "more newly" |
| :isv[čisto] | :isv[čistěje] | "more cleanly" |
| :isv[tiho] | :isv[tišeje] | "more quietly" |
| :isv[svěže] | :isv[svěžeje] | "more freshly" |

For adjectives (and their derived adverbs) whose stem ends in **:isv[-ky]**, **:isv[-eky]**, or **:isv[-oky]**, the comparative adverb is formed by adding **:isv[-je]** directly to the root, triggering iotation of the preceding consonant (§4.x):

| Positive adverb | Comparative adverb | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| :isv[daleko] | :isv[dalje] | "farther" |
| :isv[široko] | :isv[širje] | "more broadly" |
| :isv[vysoko] | :isv[vyše] | "higher" |
| :isv[blizko] | :isv[bliže] | "closer" |
| :isv[rědko] | :isv[rědže] | "more rarely" |

### 5.4.2.2. Analytic Comparative Adverb (:isv[vyše] / :isv[bolje] + positive)

The analytic comparative of adverbs is formed by placing **:isv[vyše]** or **:isv[bolje]** ("more") before the positive adverb. This construction is equivalent to the synthetic form in meaning:

- :isv[vyše prosto] — "more simply"
- :isv[bolje tiho] — "more quietly"

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source describes the analytic form as "most likely encountered" with long/foreign words and participles — an observation of tendency, not a normative SHOULD recommendation. The MAY clause for common adverbs is also editorial.
:::

The analytic form SHOULD be preferred for long words, words of foreign origin, and adverbs derived from participles. Either form MAY be used for common adverbs.

### 5.4.2.3. Superlative Adverb (:isv[naj-] prefix)

The superlative of adverbs is formed by prefixing **:isv[naj-]** to the comparative adverb:

| Positive | Comparative | Superlative | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| :isv[prosto] | :isv[prostěje] | :isv[najprostěje] | "most simply" |
| :isv[novo] | :isv[nověje] | :isv[najnověje] | "most newly" |
| :isv[daleko] | :isv[dalje] | :isv[najdalje] | "farthest" |

The prefix :isv[naj-] MAY also be applied to the analytic comparative:

- :isv[najvyše prosto] — "most simply"
- :isv[najbolje tiho] — "most quietly"

As a simplification, :isv[naj-] MAY be prefixed directly to the **positive** adverb:

- :isv[najnovo] — "most newly"
- :isv[najprosto] — "most simply"

### 5.4.2.4. Less-Than Comparative and Least Superlative

The adverb **:isv[menje]** ("less") functions as the analytic marker for the less-than comparative:

- :isv[menje prosto] — "less simply"
- :isv[menje tiho] — "less quietly"

The adverb **:isv[najmenje]** ("least") forms the least superlative:

- :isv[najmenje prosto] — "least simply"

## 5.4.3. Irregular Adverbs

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The claim that regular forms are "not incorrect" is not directly supported by the source, which instead says they "will look childish or artificial" — implying they are problematic rather than merely dispreferred.
:::

The following adverbs have suppletive or irregular comparative forms corresponding to the irregular adjective comparatives (§5.3.6.5). Use of the regular synthetic form (e.g., :isv[dobrěje]) is not incorrect, but SHOULD be avoided in favor of the established forms given below:

:::caution[Unverified Content]
In the table below, the positive adverb for "big/great" is listed as `:isv[veliko] / :isv[bolje]`, but the source gives only `:isv[veliky]` as the base adjective (positive adverb would be `:isv[veliko]`). Listing `:isv[bolje]` as a positive form is inconsistent with the source, where `:isv[bolje]` is a comparative.
:::

| Positive adverb | Comparative adverb | Gloss |
|---|---|---|
| :isv[dobro] | :isv[lěpje] / :isv[lučše] | "better" |
| :isv[zlo] | :isv[gorje] | "worse" |
| :isv[veliko] / :isv[bolje] | :isv[veče] / :isv[bolje] | "more, to a greater extent" |
| :isv[malo] | :isv[menje] | "less" |
| :isv[blago] | :isv[unje] | "more pleasantly" |
| :isv[legko] | :isv[legše] | "more easily" |
| :isv[mekko] | :isv[mekše] | "more softly" |

## 5.4.4. Additional Degree Markers

### 5.4.4.1. Excessive Degree (:isv[prě-])

The prefix **:isv[prě-]** attached to the positive form of the adjective (from which the adverb is derived) expresses an extreme or excessive degree ("very", "too", "overly"). An adverb derived from such a prefixed adjective carries the same meaning:

- :isv[prěprosty] → :isv[prěprosto] — "too simply, overly simply"

### 5.4.4.2. Negative (:isv[ne-])

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The claim that `:isv[ne-]` is "most commonly encountered in fixed lexical items" has no basis in the source material, which simply states that `:isv[ne-]` creates a negative without restricting its usage scope.
:::

The prefix **:isv[ne-]** creates a negative adverb. It is most commonly encountered in fixed lexical items:

- :isv[neprosty] → :isv[neprosto] — "not simply, in a non-simple manner"
