# Numerals
:::caution[Unverified Content]
The RFC 2119 normative framing (MUST, SHALL, SHOULD, MAY) is not present in the source material. The source uses informal language throughout. The RECOMMENDED and MUST claims in this introduction go beyond what the source states; the source simply says "it deserves recommendation to write digits instead of number words."
:::

This section specifies the forms, inflection, agreement rules, and usage conventions for all numeral categories in Interslavic. All normative statements use MUST, SHALL, SHOULD, and MAY as defined in RFC 2119.

Because numeral forms differ considerably across the individual Slavic languages — including lexically unique forms such as Russian *sorok* "forty" and *devjanosto* "ninety" — it is RECOMMENDED to write numerals as Arabic digits in written Interslavic wherever possible, so as to avoid misunderstanding. Nevertheless, the spoken and inflected forms remain necessary, and speakers MUST know how to combine numerals with the nouns they govern.

---

## Cardinal Numerals
:::caution[Unverified Content]
The structural classification into "simple," "compound," and "composite" numerals, and the further division by meaning into "definite cardinals," "fractional numerals," "collective numerals," and "indefinite cardinals," is not found in the source material.
:::

Cardinal numerals in Interslavic are divided by structure into simple (:isv[osm] "eight", :isv[sto] "hundred"), compound (:isv[petdeset] "fifty", :isv[devetdeset] "ninety"), and composite (:isv[devet sot trideset sedm] "nine hundred thirty-seven"). By meaning they are further divided into definite cardinals (treated in the relevant section), fractional numerals , collective numerals , and indefinite cardinals .

### Forms 1–10
The basic cardinal forms for zero through ten are:

| Digit | Latin | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| 0 | :isv[nula] | feminine noun, declines like :isv[žena] |
| 1 | :isv[jedin / jedna / jedno] | agrees with noun in gender, number, case; see the relevant section |
| 2 | :isv[dva] (m./n.) / :isv[dvě] (f.) | see the relevant section |
| 3 | :isv[tri] | see the relevant section |
| 4 | :isv[četyri] | see the relevant section |
| 5 | :isv[pet] | see the relevant section |
| 6 | :isv[šest] | see the relevant section |
| 7 | :isv[sedm] | see the relevant section |
| 8 | :isv[osm] | see the relevant section |
| 9 | :isv[devet] | see the relevant section |
| 10 | :isv[deset] | see the relevant section |

The neuter form of "2" is :isv[dva] in Russian, Belarusian, Ukrainian, Polish, Kashubian, and Serbo-Croatian; it is :isv[dvě] in Old Church Slavonic, Czech, Slovak, Upper and Lower Sorbian, Slovene, Macedonian, Bulgarian, and Rusyn. Both options are equally valid in Interslavic.

### Forms 11–19
The teens are formed by suffixing :isv[-nadset] (pronounced :isv[-nacet]) to the units 1–9:

| Digit | Latin |
|-------|-------|
| 11 | :isv[jedinnadset] |
| 12 | :isv[dvanadset] |
| 13 | :isv[trinadset] |
| 14 | :isv[četyrinadset] |
| 15 | :isv[petnadset] |
| 16 | :isv[šestnadset] |
| 17 | :isv[sedmnadset] |
| 18 | :isv[osmnadset] |
| 19 | :isv[devetnadset] |

### Tens: 20–90
The tens are formed by suffixing :isv[-deset] to the units 2–9:

| Digit | Latin |
|-------|-------|
| 20 | :isv[dvadeset] |
| 30 | :isv[trideset] |
| 40 | :isv[četyrideset] |
| 50 | :isv[petdeset] |
| 60 | :isv[šestdeset] |
| 70 | :isv[sedmdeset] |
| 80 | :isv[osmdeset] |
| 90 | :isv[devetdeset] |

Compound numbers from 21 to 99 are formed from high to low — tens first, then units — with the optional conjunction :isv[i] "and" between them: :isv[dvadeset (i) jedin] "21", :isv[petdeset (i) tri] "53". This direct order is characteristic of most Slavic languages.

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The following claim about reversed order in Czech, Slovak, and Slovene under German influence, and its MAY/NOT RECOMMENDED status, is not found in the source material.
:::

The reversed order (units before tens), which exists in Czech, Slovak, and Slovene under German influence, MAY be used as a stylistic variant but is NOT RECOMMENDED in standard Interslavic.

### Hundreds and thousands
:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source material presents fused forms (e.g., :isv[dvasto], :isv[tristo]) as the primary method for hundreds, with inflected forms (:isv[dvěstě], :isv[trista], :isv[petsot]) as the alternative. The doc reverses this order, presenting inflected forms as primary. Additionally, the source's fused forms differ from those in the doc (e.g., source has :isv[dvasto], doc has :isv[dvěsto]).
:::

**Hundreds (100–900):**

The primary method for hundreds is to inflect the word :isv[sto] "hundred" as a neuter noun (paradigm in the relevant section):

| Digit | Latin | Structure |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| 100 | :isv[sto] | simple |
| 200 | :isv[dvě stě] | 2 + dual of :isv[sto] |
| 300 | :isv[tri sta] | 3 + nom.pl. of :isv[sto] |
| 400 | :isv[četyri sta] | 4 + nom.pl. of :isv[sto] |
| 500 | :isv[pet sot] | 5 + gen.pl. of :isv[sto] |
| 600 | :isv[šest sot] | 6 + gen.pl. of :isv[sto] |
| 700 | :isv[sedm sot] | 7 + gen.pl. of :isv[sto] |
| 800 | :isv[osm sot] | 8 + gen.pl. of :isv[sto] |
| 900 | :isv[devet sot] | 9 + gen.pl. of :isv[sto] |

An alternative method — fusing the numeral and :isv[sto] into a single uninflected word — is also acceptable and is characteristic of Slovak, Serbo-Croatian, and Slovene:

| Digit | Fused form (Latin) |
|-------|--------------------|
| 200 | :isv[dvěsto] |
| 300 | :isv[tristo] |
| 400 | :isv[četyristo] |
| 500 | :isv[petsto] |
| 600 | :isv[šeststo] |
| 700 | :isv[sedmsto] |
| 800 | :isv[osmsto] |
| 900 | :isv[devetsto] |

When the two-part forms (e.g., :isv[dvě stě], :isv[tri sta], :isv[pet sot]) are used, both components are declined (see the relevant section). Fused forms are not declined and are treated as indeclinable nouns.

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The counting nouns :isv[sotnja] and :isv[sotka] are not mentioned in the source material.
:::

Counting by hundreds may also be expressed with the counting nouns :isv[sotnja] "a hundred (of)" and :isv[sotka], which decline as regular feminine nouns: :isv[tri sotnje] "three hundreds".

**Thousands:**

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source mentions :isv[tyseč] as masculine or feminine (gen. :isv[tyseča] or :isv[tyseči]) but does not describe a separate second-declension feminine paradigm :isv[tyseča] (like :isv[žena]), nor does it mention :isv[hiljada] as an alternative form.
:::

The word for "thousand" is :isv[tyseč]. It may be treated as a masculine noun of the first declension or as a feminine noun of the third declension (genitive :isv[tyseči]). The feminine form :isv[tyseča], declined as a second-declension feminine noun (like :isv[žena]), is also accepted and reflects East Slavic and Serbo-Croatian usage. The alternative South Slavic form :isv[hiljada] is likewise acceptable. All paradigms for :isv[tyseč] are given in the relevant section

| Qty. | Latin |
|------|-------|
| 1 000 | :isv[jedna tyseča] |
| 2 000 | :isv[dvě tyseče] |
| 3 000 | :isv[tri tyseče] |
| 4 000 | :isv[četyri tyseče] |
| 5 000 | :isv[pet tyseč] |
| 10 000 | :isv[deset tyseč] |
| 11 000 | :isv[jedinnadset tyseč] |
| 21 000 | :isv[dvadeset jedna tyseča] |

A hyphen MAY be inserted for clarity in cases like :isv[pet-tyseč] "5 000", though this is not required.

**Millions and milliards:**

:isv[Milion] "million" (10⁶) and :isv[miliard] "milliard / billion" (10⁹) are masculine nouns declined like :isv[dom].

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The discussion of short scale vs. long scale, the SHOULD recommendations about preferring :isv[milion]/:isv[miliard], and the entire scale names table (including :isv[bilion], :isv[trilion], :isv[kvadrilion]) are not found in the source material.
:::

The larger scale terms (:isv[bilion], :isv[trilion], etc.) exist in Interslavic but their precise value depends on whether the short scale (used in Russian, Ukrainian, Belarusian, Bulgarian, Serbo-Croatian) or the long scale (used in Polish, Slovak, Czech, Slovene) is assumed. Up to 999 999 999 999, the terms :isv[milion] and :isv[miliard] are unambiguous across all Slavic languages and SHOULD be preferred; for larger numbers, numerals SHOULD be written as digits.

The number scale names are:

| Name (Latin) | Short scale | Long scale |
|--------------|-------------|------------|
| :isv[tyseč / tyseča] | 10³ | 10³ |
| :isv[milion] | 10⁶ | 10⁶ |
| :isv[miliard] | 10⁹ | 10⁹ |
| :isv[bilion] | 10⁹ | 10¹² |
| :isv[trilion] | 10¹² | 10¹⁸ |
| :isv[kvadrilion] | 10¹⁵ | 10²⁴ |

### Composite numbers and word order
Composite numbers are always formed from high to low: thousands — hundreds — tens — units. The word :isv[i] "and" MAY be inserted between tens and units: :isv[tri-tyseč četyristo petdeset (i) šest] "3 456".

For agreement of composite numbers with their dependent nouns, the rightmost component governs the agreement (see the relevant section).

### Indefinite cardinal numerals
Apart from the definite cardinals, Interslavic has a category of indefinite cardinal numerals:

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| :isv[mnogo] | much, many |
| :isv[malo] | little, few |
| :isv[velje] | a lot |
| :isv[veče] | more |
| :isv[menje] | less |
| :isv[koliko] | how many |
| :isv[nekoliko] | a few, some |
| :isv[toliko] | this/that many |
| :isv[para] | a few |

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The claim that :isv[malo] and :isv[pol] are not declined, and that all other indefinite cardinals govern the genitive plural, is not stated in the source material. The source simply lists the indefinite cardinal forms without specifying their declension or agreement behavior.
:::

The words :isv[malo] and :isv[pol] "half" are not declined. All other indefinite cardinals are declined and govern the genitive plural of the noun they quantify, as the numerals 5 and above do.

## Numeral–Noun Agreement
The case and number form of a noun following a cardinal numeral depends on the value of the numeral. Agreement patterns differ sharply between the numerals 1–4 and all higher numerals.

### 1: full agreement with noun
The numeral :isv[jedin] "one" agrees with its noun in gender, number, and case, exactly as an adjective does. Examples:

- :isv[jedin dobry prijatelj] "one good friend" (masc. sg.)
- :isv[jedna mlada žena] "one young woman" (fem. sg.)
- :isv[jedno malo děte] "one small child" (neut. sg.)
- :isv[jedne sinje džinsy] "one pair of jeans" (plurale tantum)

The numeral :isv[jedin] is not restricted to its purely numerical sense; it is also used in the meanings "a certain", "lone", "individual", "single", making it even more adjective-like in behaviour.

### 2–4: nominative plural of noun
The numerals :isv[dva], :isv[tri], :isv[četyri], :isv[oba], and :isv[obydva] govern the **nominative plural** of the following noun (and its modifying adjective) in the nominative and accusative cases. In oblique cases, these numerals agree with the noun in case. Examples:

- :isv[dva věrni drugi] "two faithful friends" (nom.)
- :isv[tri lětnje měsěce] "three summer months" (nom.)
- :isv[četyri dolge dni] "four long days" (nom.)

In the genitive and other oblique cases:

- :isv[dvoh věrnyh drugov] "of two faithful friends"
- :isv[trěh lětnjih měsěcev] "of three summer months"

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source mentions that "some languages, notably Serbo-Croat and Russian, prefer the genitive singular instead" after 2-4, but does not use MAY/NOT RECOMMENDED normative language. The source simply recommends the nominative plural "for reasons of clarity."
:::

The Russian and Serbo-Croatian pattern of using the genitive singular after 2–4 (cf. Russ. *dva vernogo druga*) MAY be used as a variant but is NOT RECOMMENDED in standard Interslavic.

### 5 and above: genitive plural of noun
The numerals :isv[pet] and all higher numerals govern the **genitive plural** of the following noun and its modifiers in the nominative and accusative. In oblique cases, the numeral itself is declined and the dependent noun stands in the plural of the appropriate case. Two patterns exist:

**Pattern A (numerals 5–99 and compound numbers whose rightmost element is 5–99):** In the nominative/accusative, the numeral governs genitive plural; in oblique cases, the numeral is in the singular (as a noun-like form) and the dependent noun is in the corresponding plural case.

| Case | "5 important words" | "7 famous people" | "10 rubles" |
|------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Nom./Acc. | :isv[pet vaznyh slov] | :isv[sedm znamenityh ljudij] | :isv[deset russkyh rubljev] |
| Gen. | :isv[peti vaznyh slov] | :isv[sedmi znamenityh ljudij] | :isv[deseti russkyh rubljev] |
| Loc. | :isv[(o) peti vaznyh slovah] | :isv[(o) sedmi znamenityh ljudjah] | :isv[(o) deseti russkyh rubljah] |
| Dat. | :isv[peti vaznym slovam] | :isv[sedmi znamenitym ljudjam] | :isv[deseti russkim rubljam] |
| Ins. | :isv[petju vaznymi slovami] | :isv[sedmju znamenitymi ljudjami] | :isv[desetju russkimi rubljevami] |

**Pattern B (sto, tyseč, milion, miliard):** These nouns govern genitive plural of their dependents in ALL cases (including oblique), because they function as true nouns.

| Case | "100 pupils" | "1 000 yen" |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| Nom./Acc. | :isv[sto usrdnyh učenikov] | :isv[tyseča japonskih jenov] |
| Gen. | :isv[sta usrdnyh učenikov] | :isv[tyseče japonskih jenov] |
| Loc. | :isv[(o) stu usrdnyh učenikov] | :isv[(o) tyseči japonskih jenov] |
| Dat. | :isv[stu usrdnyh učenikov] | :isv[tyseči japonskim jenom] |
| Ins. | :isv[stom usrdnyh učenikov] | :isv[tyseččeju japonskih jenov] |

**Composite numbers:** For composite cardinals such as :isv[dvadeset jedin] "21" or :isv[petdeset tri] "53", the rightmost element determines the agreement pattern:

| Case | "21 teachers" | "53 people" | "67 workers" |
|------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Nom. | :isv[dvadeset (i) jedin učitelj] | :isv[petdeset (i) tri ljudi] | :isv[šestdeset (i) sedm rabotnikov] |
| Acc. | :isv[dvadeset (i) jednogo učitelja] | :isv[petdeset (i) trěh ljudij] | :isv[šestdeset (i) sedm rabotnikov] |
| Gen. | :isv[dvadeseti (i) jednogo učitelja] | :isv[petdeseti (i) trěh ljudij] | :isv[šestdeseti (i) sedmi rabotnikov] |
| Dat. | :isv[dvadeseti (i) jednomu učitelju] | :isv[petdeseti (i) trěm ljudjam] | :isv[šestdeseti (i) sedmi rabotnikam] |
| Ins. | :isv[dvadesetju (i) jednym učiteljem] | :isv[petdesetju (i) trěmi ljudjami] | :isv[šestdesetju (i) sedmju rabotnikami] |

## Declension of Numerals
### Declension of :isv[jedin] (1)
The numeral :isv[jedin] is declined like a hard adjective (:isv[dobry]-type) for all forms except the masculine nominative singular animate, which ends in :isv[-in] rather than :isv[-y]. It has both singular and plural paradigms (plural for pluralia tantum nouns and distributive uses).

| Case | Masc. sg. anim. | Masc. sg. inan. | Neut. sg. | Fem. sg. | Pl. (all genders) |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Nom. | :isv[jedin] | :isv[jedin] | :isv[jedno] | :isv[jedna] | :isv[jedni] (m.), :isv[jedne] (f./n.) |
| Acc. | :isv[jednogo] | :isv[jedin] | :isv[jedno] | :isv[jednu] | :isv[jednyh] (m.anim.), :isv[jedne] (others) |
| Gen. | :isv[jednogo] | :isv[jednogo] | :isv[jednogo] | :isv[jednoj] | :isv[jednyh] |
| Dat. | :isv[jednomu] | :isv[jednomu] | :isv[jednomu] | :isv[jednoj] | :isv[jednym] |
| Ins. | :isv[jednym] | :isv[jednym] | :isv[jednym] | :isv[jednoju] | :isv[jednymi] |
| Loc. | :isv[(o) jednom] | :isv[(o) jednom] | :isv[(o) jednom] | :isv[(o) jednoj] | :isv[(o) jednyh] |

### Declension of :isv[dva] (2), :isv[oba], :isv[obydva]
These three numerals are declined together because they share the same paradigm. Only :isv[dva] and :isv[obydva] have a gender distinction in the nominative/accusative (masculine/neuter vs. feminine). The endings preserve remnants of the ancient dual number.

**Standard plural-based declension (preferred):**

| Case | :isv[dva] m./n. | :isv[dva] f. (:isv[dvě]) | :isv[oba] m./n. | :isv[oba] f. (:isv[obě]) | :isv[obydva] m./n. | :isv[obydva] f. (:isv[obydvě]) |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|------------------------|
| Nom. | :isv[dva] | :isv[dvě] | :isv[oba] | :isv[obě] | :isv[obydva] | :isv[obydvě] |
| Acc. | :isv[dvoh] (anim.) / :isv[dva] (inan.) | :isv[dvě] | :isv[oboh] (anim.) / :isv[oba] (inan.) | :isv[obě] | :isv[obydvoh] (anim.) / :isv[obydva] (inan.) | :isv[obydvě] |
| Gen. | :isv[dvoh] | :isv[dvoh] | :isv[oboh] | :isv[oboh] | :isv[obydvoh] | :isv[obydvoh] |
| Dat. | :isv[dvom] | :isv[dvom] | :isv[obom] | :isv[obom] | :isv[obydvom] | :isv[obydvom] |
| Ins. | :isv[dvoma] | :isv[dvoma] | :isv[oboma] | :isv[oboma] | :isv[obydvoma] | :isv[obydvoma] |
| Loc. | :isv[dvoh] | :isv[dvoh] | :isv[oboh] | :isv[oboh] | :isv[obydvoh] | :isv[obydvoh] |

**Dual-based declension (optional):**

Interslavic permits the use of dual forms for :isv[dva], :isv[oba], and :isv[obydva]. In this paradigm, the neuter nominative/accusative coincides with the feminine form (i.e., only :isv[dvě], :isv[obě], :isv[obydvě] are used for neuter, not :isv[dva], :isv[oba], :isv[obydva]). The dual-based declension of :isv[dva] documented by Lombino (2021) distinguishes gender in the dative/instrumental as well:

| Case | Masculine | Feminine/Neuter | All genders |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Nom. | :isv[dva] | :isv[dvě] | — |
| Acc. inan. | = Nom. | = Nom. | — |
| Acc. anim. | :isv[dvu] | — | — |
| Gen./Loc. | — | — | :isv[dvu] |
| Dat./Instr. | :isv[dvoma] | :isv[dvěma] | — |

This paradigm directly mirrors the dual case-merging pattern found in nouns: nominative = accusative (with animate exception), genitive = locative, dative = instrumental. See the nouns chapter for the full optional dual number paradigm.

The endings of these numerals resemble adjectival plural endings, which is natural given that they originally were adjectival in function.

### Declension of :isv[tri] (3) and :isv[četyri] (4)
The numerals :isv[tri] and :isv[četyri] do not distinguish gender. They are declined similarly to the plural of adjectives.

| Case | :isv[tri] | :isv[četyri] |
|------|---------------|----------------------|
| Nom. | :isv[tri] | :isv[četyri] |
| Acc. | :isv[trěh] (anim.) / :isv[tri] (inan.) | :isv[četyrěh] (anim.) / :isv[četyri] (inan.) |
| Gen. | :isv[trěh] | :isv[četyrěh] |
| Dat. | :isv[trěm] | :isv[četyrěm] |
| Ins. | :isv[trěmi] | :isv[četyrěmi] |
| Loc. | :isv[(o) trěh] | :isv[(o) četyrěh] |

### Declension of numerals 5–99
The numerals from :isv[pet] upwards are inflected as nouns of the :isv[kost]-type (third-declension feminine). Only one number (nominative = accusative) is used throughout; the form for all oblique cases other than instrumental is identical with the genitive.

**Paradigm for selected numerals:**

| Case | :isv[pet] (5) | :isv[šest] (6) | :isv[sedm] (7) | :isv[osm] (8) | :isv[devet] (9) | :isv[deset] (10) |
|------|-----------|------------|------------|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Nom./Acc. | :isv[pet] | :isv[šest] | :isv[sedm] | :isv[osm] | :isv[devet] | :isv[deset] |
| Gen./Dat./Loc. | :isv[peti] | :isv[šesti] | :isv[sedmi] | :isv[osmi] | :isv[deveti] | :isv[deseti] |
| Ins. | :isv[petju] | :isv[šestju] | :isv[sedmju] | :isv[osmju] | :isv[devetju] | :isv[desetju] |

For compound tens (50–90), only the second element (:isv[-deset]) changes in declension; the first element (the unit) remains in its base form:

| Case | :isv[petdeset] (50) | :isv[sedmdeset] (70) | :isv[devetdeset] (90) |
|------|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Nom./Acc. | :isv[petdeset] | :isv[sedmdeset] | :isv[devetdeset] |
| Gen./Dat./Loc. | :isv[petdeseti] | :isv[sedmdeseti] | :isv[devetdeseti] |
| Ins. | :isv[petdesetju] | :isv[sedmdesetju] | :isv[devetdesetju] |

For the teens (11–19), the entire compound word is declined as a :isv[kost]-type noun: :isv[jedinnadset], gen. :isv[jedinnadseti].

### Declension of :isv[sto] (100)
The numeral :isv[sto] declines as a neuter noun (like :isv[slovo]) with singular, plural, and dual forms. The dual is used in the numeral :isv[dvě stě] "200"; the plural nominative :isv[sta] is used for 300–400; the genitive plural :isv[sot] is used for 500–900.

| Case | Singular | Plural | Dual |
|------|----------|--------|------|
| Nom./Acc. | :isv[sto] | :isv[sta] | :isv[stě] |
| Gen. | :isv[sta] | :isv[sot] | :isv[stu] |
| Dat. | :isv[stu] | :isv[stam] | :isv[stama] |
| Ins. | :isv[stom] | :isv[stami] | :isv[stama] |
| Loc. | :isv[(o) stu] | :isv[(o) stah] | :isv[(o) stu] |

## Ordinal Numerals
Ordinal numerals indicate the position of an item in a sequence. Grammatically they are adjectives and MUST agree with the noun they modify in gender, number, and case.

All ordinals except :isv[trětji] are declined as hard adjectives (:isv[dobry]-type; see the relevant section). The ordinal :isv[trětji] follows the soft adjectival declension (:isv[svěži]-type; see the relevant section).

### Irregular ordinals 1st–4th
| Cardinal | Latin ordinal |
|----------|---------------|
| 1 | :isv[prvy] |
| 2 | :isv[vtory] or :isv[drugy] |
| 3 | :isv[trětji] |
| 4 | :isv[četvrty] |

### Regular ordinals 5th and above
Ordinals from 5th upward are formed by adding the hard adjectival ending :isv[-y] (with appropriate gender/number/case inflection) directly to the cardinal form:

| Cardinal | Ordinal (Latin) |
|----------|-----------------|
| :isv[pet] | :isv[pety] |
| :isv[šest] | :isv[šesty] |
| :isv[sedm] | :isv[sedmy] |
| :isv[osm] | :isv[osmy] |
| :isv[devet] | :isv[devety] |
| :isv[deset] | :isv[desety] |
| :isv[jedinnadset] | :isv[jedinnadsety] |
| :isv[dvadeset] | :isv[dvadesety] |
| :isv[petdeset] | :isv[petdesety] |

For hundreds, two patterns are acceptable:

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source only mentions :isv[sotny] or :isv[stoty] for "100th." The extended forms for 200th, 500th, etc. (:isv[dvasotny], :isv[petsotny], :isv[dvastoty], :isv[petstoty]) are not in the source.

- Suffix :isv[-sotny]: :isv[sotny] "100th", :isv[dvasotny] "200th", :isv[petsotny] "500th"
- Suffix :isv[-stoty]: :isv[stoty] "100th", :isv[dvastoty] "200th", :isv[petstoty] "500th"

For thousands: :isv[tysečny] "1000th".
:::caution[Unverified Content]
The forms :isv[milionny] and :isv[miliardny] are not mentioned in the source material.
:::

For millions: :isv[milionny] "millionth".
For milliards: :isv[miliardny] "milliardth".

### Composite ordinals
In a composite ordinal numeral, **only the last element** is declined as an ordinal; all preceding elements remain as cardinals in their nominative form:

:isv[v tyseč devetsot osmdeset četvrtom godu] "in the year 1984".

:::caution[Unverified Content]
This composite ordinal example is not in the source material.
:::

:isv[jedna tyseča dvě stě petdeset prvy] "one thousand two hundred fifty-first".

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The convention of indicating ordinals with a digit followed by a period, and the example sentence, are not found in the source material.
:::

When written with digits, ordinals are indicated by a digit followed by a period (even when not at the end of a sentence): :isv[Aleksandr ima den rodenija 1. januara] "Aleksandr's birthday is January 1st."

## Collective Numerals
Collective numerals refer to members of a closed group and can be translated as "a group of" or "-some". They are:

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| :isv[dvoje] | twosome, pair |
| :isv[troje] | threesome |
| :isv[četvero] | group of four |
| :isv[petero] | group of five |
| :isv[šestero] | group of six |
| :isv[sedmero] | group of seven |
| :isv[osmero] | group of eight |
| :isv[devetero] | group of nine |
| :isv[desetero] | group of ten |
| :isv[oboje] | both (mixed gender) |

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source says collective numerals "can be used" in these situations but does not use SHOULD normative language.
:::

Collective numerals SHOULD be used instead of cardinal numerals in the following situations:

1. When the group consists of both men and women: :isv[dva studenti] "two male students", :isv[dvě studentky] "two female students", :isv[dvoje studentov] "two students (mixed)".
2. With gender-neutral words like :isv[ljudi] and :isv[děti]: :isv[osmero ljudij] "eight people", :isv[četvero dětij] "four children".
3. With neuter words denoting young animals (singular :isv[-e], plural :isv[-eta]): :isv[troje telet] "three calves".
4. For objects that naturally come in pairs: :isv[dvoje rukavic] "two gloves".
5. With pluralia tantum (words existing only in the plural): :isv[dvoje dverij] "two doors".

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source states the verb is "in the 3rd person singular (neuter)" but does not use MUST normative language.
:::

The noun following a collective numeral is always in the **genitive plural**. If this noun phrase is the subject of a clause, the verb MUST be in the **3rd person singular neuter**. Collective numerals may also be used without a noun: :isv[my oboje] "both of us (m. + f.)", :isv[zabava v troje] "a party for three".

## Further Numeral-Based Word Classes
### Multiplicative adjectives
The stems of collective numerals serve as the base for multiplicative (aggregative) adjectives meaning "X-fold":

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| :isv[jediny] | only, single |
| :isv[dvojny] | double, twofold |
| :isv[trojny] | treble, threefold |
| :isv[četverny] | quadruple, fourfold |
| :isv[peterny] | quintuple |
| :isv[šesterny] | sextuple |

With the prefix :isv[po-], these adjectives become verbs meaning "to multiply by X": :isv[podvojiti] "to double", :isv[potrojiti] "to triple", :isv[početveriti] "to multiply by four". The prefix :isv[raz-] produces the converse: :isv[razdvojiti] "to split in two", :isv[raztrojiti] "to split into three".

### Multiplicative adverbs
Multiplicative adverbs are formed by adding :isv[-kratno] to the cardinal stem:

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| :isv[jednokratno] | once, one time |
| :isv[dvakratno] | twice |
| :isv[trikratno] | three times |
| :isv[četyrikratno] | four times |
| :isv[petkratno] | five times |
| :isv[mnogokratno] | many times |
| :isv[nekolicokratno] | a few times |

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source uses the form :isv[několikokratno] for "a few times", not :isv[nekolicokratno] as written here.

### Differential (generic) numerals
Differential numerals indicate qualitative differentiation — "of different types" or "of how many kinds". The older form uses the collective stem directly: :isv[dvoj], :isv[troj] (declined like the possessive pronoun :isv[moj]).

The more common form uses the suffix :isv[-aky]:

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| :isv[dvojaky] | of two kinds |
| :isv[trojaky] | of three kinds |
| :isv[četveraky] | of four kinds |
| :isv[peteraky] | of five kinds |
| :isv[kolikoraky] | of how many kinds |

When adverbialised, the meaning becomes "in X ways": :isv[trojako] "in three ways".

### Fractions
For the word "half", Interslavic uses :isv[pol] (indeclinable) or its full noun form :isv[polovina] / :isv[polovica] (declined as a feminine noun). :isv[Pol] MAY also be used as a prefix: :isv[pol-mrtvy] "half-dead".

All other fractions are formed by replacing the ordinal ending :isv[-y] with :isv[-ina]:

| Fraction | Latin |
|----------|-------|
| 1/3 | :isv[tretjina] |
| 1/4 | :isv[četvrtina] |
| 1/5 | :isv[petina] |
| 1/6 | :isv[šestina] |
| 1/7 | :isv[sedmina] |
| 1/8 | :isv[osmina] |
| 1/9 | :isv[devetina] |
| 1/10 | :isv[desetina] |
| 1/16 | :isv[šestnadsetina] |
| 1/100 | :isv[sotina] or :isv[stotina] |

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The source gives the form as :isv[sedm trideseti-osmin] (with the genitive form :isv[trideseti-]), not :isv[sedm trideset-osmin] as written here.
:::

These fraction nouns are feminine and are declined as second-declension nouns. Thus "7/38" is :isv[sedm trideset-osmin] (the hyphen enhances clarity).

Ordinal-based fractions (e.g., :isv[dvě trětje] "two thirds") are also used, reflecting the pattern common across most Slavic languages.

### Substantivised numerals
When a numeral is used as a noun (referring to a digit, a bus line, a school grade, a playing card, a banknote, etc.), the following forms apply:

For 2–4, the collective numeral stem plus :isv[-ka] is used: :isv[dvojka] "a two", :isv[trojka] "a three", :isv[četverka] "a four".

For all other numerals, :isv[-ka] is appended to the cardinal form: :isv[jedinka] "a one", :isv[petka] "a five", :isv[šestka] "a six", etc.

:::caution[Unverified Content]
The claim that "not all forms in :isv[-ica] are equally established" and that "forms in :isv[-ka] are preferred" is not stated in the source material. The source simply presents both suffixes as alternatives.
:::

Alternatively, the suffix :isv[-ica] MAY be used: :isv[jedinica], :isv[petica], :isv[šestica], :isv[sedmica], etc. Not all forms in :isv[-ica] are equally established; forms in :isv[-ka] are preferred.
